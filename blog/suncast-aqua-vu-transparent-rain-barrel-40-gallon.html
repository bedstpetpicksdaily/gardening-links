Frequently Asked Questions

Does the transparent plastic actually stay clear enough to see the water level?

Yes, initially it's crystal clear. After 2-3 seasons in direct sunlight, the clarity fades to a frosted appearance, but you can still see the water level inside—just less sharply. Placing it in partial shade slows this degradation noticeably. If pristine transparency is essential long-term, consider a premium polycarbonate model, though you'll pay 40-60% more.

Will mosquitoes breed in this barrel?

Only if you skip the mesh screen that comes with it. The transparent design actually helps here—you can visually inspect for larvae every few days. I've never had mosquito issues with the screen properly installed, even through humid July and August months. Change the screen if it tears, and ensure the overflow outlet is also screened.

How much water does a typical rainfall actually collect?

A half-inch rain event over a standard 55-square-foot roof area yields roughly 15-17 gallons. A one-inch rain fills the 40-gallon barrel completely on a typical roof. During July's occasional downpours, you'll fill this barrel in 30-45 minutes of moderate rain. For drought periods or light showers, expect 5-8 gallons per event.

Is 40 gallons enough for a real garden?

It depends on your garden size and watering frequency. A 100-square-foot raised bed needs roughly 60 gallons for a deep weekly watering. With one 40-gallon barrel, you're supplementing municipal water, not replacing it entirely. Most gardeners find they need 2-3 barrels for genuine self-sufficiency, or one barrel if you're only supplementing during rainy seasons.

How does it compare to the similar-priced alternatives?

The main competitor in this price range is the Rubbermaid rain barrel, which costs about the same but uses opaque plastic and holds 50 gallons. You lose visibility but gain 10 gallons of capacity. For $20 more, some brands offer insulated options better for harsh climates. The Suncast wins on ease of setup and monitoring; alternatives win on slight capacity advantages or durability claims.
