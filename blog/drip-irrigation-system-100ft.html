Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to install a 100ft drip irrigation system?

Most DIY installations take 1-2 hours depending on garden layout complexity. Simply lay out the main line, attach drip tubing, add emitters at plant locations, and connect to your water source. No special tools or plumbing knowledge required.

Can I expand a 100ft drip irrigation kit?

Yes, most systems are expandable. You can add additional tubing, emitters, and connectors to extend coverage or create new zones as your garden grows. Check compatibility before purchasing expansion kits.

What water pressure do drip systems need?

Most 100ft drip irrigation systems work best with 20-60 PSI water pressure. If your water pressure is too high, use a pressure regulator. If it's too low, the system may not distribute water evenly across the full 100ft length.

How often should I run my drip irrigation system?

Watering frequency depends on plant type, soil, climate, and season. Most gardens need 1-3 watering sessions per week. Run your system early morning or evening to minimize evaporation and maximize plant absorption.
