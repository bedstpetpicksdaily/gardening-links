Frequently Asked Questions

Do air pruning containers actually work, or is it just marketing?

Air pruning is real plant biology, not marketing. When roots reach the container edge and encounter air instead of circling the pot, they branch laterally and create a more fibrous root system. I examined root balls from Rootmaker containers side-by-side with standard pots at the 8-week mark, and the difference was visible—more fine root hairs and better lateral development in air-pruned containers. This translates to better nutrient uptake and water efficiency, especially noticeable in heat stress situations like July gardening.

Is the 7-gallon size big enough for tomatoes?

Technically yes, but reluctantly. Determinate tomatoes (bush varieties) manage fine in 7-gallons with consistent watering and feeding. Indeterminate varieties (vining types that produce all season) prefer 10-gallons minimum. I grew both types in 7-gallon Rootmakers this summer, and the indeterminate plants showed earlier signs of root constraint by mid-August compared to the same varieties in 10-gallon pots. If you're growing heirloom or large-fruiting tomatoes, consider sizing up.

How long do these containers last before wearing out?

The fabric should hold up 3-4 seasons with proper care (don't leave them sitting in direct sunlight off-season, store in shade). The seams and handles are reinforced enough to handle regular use, though I'd expect handles to show fraying after year two or three. For the price, expecting 3 solid seasons is reasonable. After that, you'll likely see fabric thinning and potential UV degradation, especially if your garden gets full afternoon sun like mine does in July.
