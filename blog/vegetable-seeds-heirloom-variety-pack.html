Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between heirloom and hybrid vegetable seeds?

Heirloom seeds are open-pollinated varieties passed down through generations, allowing you to save seeds for future planting. Hybrid seeds are bred from two different parent plants and don't produce viable seeds for replanting, but often have faster growth and higher yields.

Can I grow heirloom seeds in containers and raised beds?

Yes, most heirloom varieties grow well in containers and raised garden beds. Choose compact or determinate varieties for container gardening, and ensure adequate soil depth, drainage, and irrigation for best results.

Are heirloom vegetable seeds non-GMO?

Yes, all true heirloom seeds are non-GMO and open-pollinated. However, verify product descriptions confirm non-GMO status, as some seed companies may misuse the 'heirloom' label.

How do I store heirloom vegetable seeds long-term?

Store seeds in a cool, dry place at 40-50°F with low humidity. Use airtight containers, add desiccant packets, and keep them away from direct sunlight. Properly stored seeds remain viable for 3-5 years or longer.
