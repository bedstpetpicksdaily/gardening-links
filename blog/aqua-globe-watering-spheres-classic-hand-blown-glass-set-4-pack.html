Frequently Asked Questions

How long does one Aqua Globe sphere actually water a plant?

Realistically 7-14 days, with major variables determining where your plant falls in that range. Sandy soil drains faster and empties the bulb in 7-10 days. Clay-heavy soil retains moisture longer, stretching that to 12-14 days. Temperature matters enormously—hot July weather speeds evaporation and accelerates water release from the soil. High-humidity indoor environments slow the process considerably. The best approach: test one sphere on a plant you monitor closely before relying on a set of four for your month-long vacation.

Will these work for both indoor houseplants and outdoor containers?

Yes, with one important caveat about outdoor placement. The glass is durable enough for outdoor use, but the aesthetic advantage—the reason you're paying premium prices—disappears once you bury most of the sphere in soil. Outdoors, they work best in larger decorative pots where the glass remains visible, or in hanging baskets where the sphere dangles visibly. For your vegetable garden beds, plastic alternatives perform identically at half the cost. Use the Aqua Globes where people will actually see them.

How do I refill and reuse them between waterings?

Fill with distilled water or tap water (chlorine typically doesn't damage plants at household levels). Insert the narrow stem into moist soil at a 45-degree angle—pushing straight down risks soil compaction. The water releases as soil dries out and pulls away from the sphere. For reuse, rinse thoroughly with water and let air-dry completely before storage. Store in a cushioned container (not loose in a garden shed) to minimize breakage risk. This reusability factor is why the higher upfront cost eventually amortizes across five to ten seasons of use.
