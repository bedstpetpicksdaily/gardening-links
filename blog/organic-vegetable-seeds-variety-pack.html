Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between heirloom and hybrid vegetable seeds?

Heirloom seeds are open-pollinated varieties passed down for generations, allowing you to save seeds for next year. Hybrid seeds are bred from specific parent plants and produce uniform crops but cannot be reliably saved. Organic packs often emphasize heirlooms for sustainability.

Can I use vegetable seeds variety packs in containers and raised beds?

Yes, most vegetable seeds in variety packs work well in both containers and raised beds. Choose compact varieties like bush beans, lettuce, and patio tomatoes for planters, and larger vegetables like cabbage and squash for raised garden beds.

How long do organic vegetable seeds stay viable?

Most organic vegetable seeds remain viable for 2-5 years when stored in a cool, dry place. Check the package date before purchasing and store seeds in airtight containers away from moisture and heat for maximum longevity.

Do organic seeds require different watering or irrigation setup?

No, organic seeds have the same watering requirements as conventional seeds. They work perfectly with standard drip irrigation systems, soaker hoses, and hand-watering techniques for your garden.
