Frequently Asked Questions

How deep should I bury the 1/2" mainline?

Raindrip recommends 6-12 inches deep depending on your climate and soil. In my setup, I went 8 inches in clay-heavy soil and 6 inches in amended raised beds. Shallower burial warms faster in spring but risks exposure during dry summers when soil settles. Deeper installation stays more stable year-round but requires more initial digging. July is honestly not ideal for burying lines if you can avoid it—wait until late August if this is your first system.

Can this system handle slopes or uneven terrain?

Yes, but with caveats. The 1/2" tubing can handle moderate slopes if you install pressure regulators at high points and drip emitters along the downslope sections. I tested it on a 15-degree slope in my test bed and got acceptable distribution, though the lower sections watered slightly faster due to gravity. For slopes steeper than 20 degrees, you'll want to install multiple zones with separate regulators.

What's the real difference between Raindrip Professional and budget alternatives I see for half the price?
