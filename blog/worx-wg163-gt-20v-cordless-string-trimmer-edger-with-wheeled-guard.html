Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Wheeled guard is genuinely smart engineering—reduces strain on your wrists during extended use and gives you consistent edge depth without having to constantly adjust your stance. This feature alone separates it from stripped-down competitors and explains much of its positive review volume.
  • ✓ 20V battery system is reliable and integrates with the broader WORX ecosystem, meaning if you already own their drills or blowers, you're buying into compatibility rather than creating another power tool island in your garage.
  • ✓ Lightweight design (weighs less than 5 lbs without battery) makes it accessible for older users and those doing frequent touch-ups without fatigue becoming the limiting factor in your session length.
  • ✓ String head rotates between trimmer and edger modes with a simple lever adjustment—no tools required, and the transition is fast enough that you'll actually use both functions rather than choosing one and sticking with it.
Cons
  • ✗ Runtime per charge maxes out around 45-60 minutes on the standard battery, which sounds adequate until you're trimming a half-acre lot and realize you're charging mid-project. July heat also degrades battery performance noticeably compared to spring conditions.
  • ✗ The 12-inch cutting width is narrow—you're covering less ground per pass than a 16-inch competitor, turning larger properties into a tedious series of back-and-forth passes that eat time you could spend on actual gardening instead.

Our Verdict

The WORX WG163 GT deserves its 4.3-star rating because it does one job well: maintaining small to medium yards with minimal physical strain and zero gas fumes. At its typical price point, it's priced fairly for what you get—a complete trimming and edging system without the learning curve of gas models. However, it's not a universal solution. If you have more than a half-acre, need marathon runtime, or prioritize speed over convenience, you'll outgrow this quickly and resent the limitations. For homeowners with modest properties who value ease of use and want to avoid gas maintenance, this trimmer justifies its cost entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the 20V battery actually last, and is it enough for one mowing session?

Expect 45-60 minutes of active trimming on a full charge with standard battery. For most quarter-acre to half-acre residential lots, that's one complete session—but not if you're also edging hardscaping and doing detail work. Summer heat (like July conditions) can reduce this by 10-15%. WORX does sell extended batteries, but that's additional investment. If your property requires more than an hour of trimming work, budget for a second battery or plan two sessions.

Can this actually handle thick weeds and overgrown areas, or just light maintenance?

The WORX WG163 GT excels at regular maintenance—weekly or bi-weekly cutting of normal lawn growth. Throw it at thick weeds, brambles, or neglected patches and it struggles noticeably. The string head works best when you're preventing overgrowth rather than conquering it. This is a 'use it to stay on top of your yard' tool, not a 'rescue my neglected property' solution.

Is the wheeled guard actually useful, or is it gimmicky marketing?

It's genuinely useful. The wheels ride along the ground and set your cutting depth automatically, which eliminates the wrist-angle guessing game that causes fatigue on standard guards. You won't appreciate it until you've used a trimmer without one for 20 minutes—then you'll understand why it appears in nearly every positive review. That said, wheels can occasionally jam with grass debris, requiring quick rinse-outs in humid conditions.
