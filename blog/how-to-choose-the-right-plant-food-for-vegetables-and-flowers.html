Understanding NPK Ratios and Nutrient Needs

Every plant food label displays three numbers representing the NPK ratio: Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K). Nitrogen promotes leafy, green growth; phosphorus supports root development and flowering; potassium strengthens overall plant health and disease resistance. Vegetables typically benefit from balanced or nitrogen-rich formulas (higher first number), while flowering plants often need more phosphorus and potassium (higher middle and last numbers). Understanding these ratios helps you select a product tailored to your specific plants' needs.

Organic vs Synthetic Plant Foods: What's the Difference?

Organic plant foods, derived from natural sources like compost, bone meal, and seaweed, release nutrients slowly and improve soil structure over time. Synthetic fertilizers provide immediate nutrient availability, perfect for plants showing deficiency symptoms. Elixir Garden Supplies offers both options, allowing gardeners to choose based on their gardening philosophy and timeline. Organic options are ideal for long-term soil health, while synthetic solutions work best for quick nutrient boosts during growing season.

Choosing Between Granular, Liquid, and Slow-Release Formulas

Plant food comes in three main forms: granular (mixed into soil), liquid (dissolved in water), and slow-release pellets (nutrients release gradually). Granular products suit vegetable gardens and seasonal feeding, liquid formulas work well for container plants and quick absorption, and slow-release options minimise feeding frequency. Elixir Garden Supplies stocks a comprehensive range of all three formats, making it easy to find solutions for every gardening situation and plant type.

Specialty Formulas for Specific Plants

Some plants have unique nutritional requirements. Tomatoes benefit from extra potassium, roses need consistent phosphorus, and acid-loving plants like blueberries require different pH levels. Many gardeners find success with specialty formulations designed for specific plant groups. Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I feed my vegetables and flowers?

Most vegetables benefit from feeding every 2-3 weeks during growing season, while flowering plants typically need weekly or bi-weekly applications. Slow-release fertilizers from Elixir Garden Supplies reduce feeding frequency to once per season. Always follow package instructions for your specific product.

Can I use the same plant food for vegetables and flowers?

While balanced formulas work for both, specialized products yield better results. Vegetables prefer nitrogen-rich foods for leafy growth, while flowers need higher phosphorus and potassium for blooms. Elixir Garden Supplies offers both general-purpose and specialty formulations for optimal outcomes.

What's the difference between fertilizer and plant food?

The terms are often used interchangeably. Fertilizer is the product itself, while plant food refers to the nutrients it provides. Both supply essential minerals (nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium) that plants need for healthy growth, available in organic and synthetic varieties from Elixir Garden Supplies.

Is organic plant food better than synthetic?

Both have advantages. Organic improves soil structure and provides slow, steady nutrients ideal for long-term health. Synthetic delivers immediate results for nutrient deficiencies. The best choice depends on your goals—Elixir Garden Supplies stocks both types so you can choose what suits your garden best.

How do I know if my plants need more fertilizer?

Signs include pale leaves, slow growth, fewer flowers, and poor fruit production. However, over-fertilizing causes burning, salt buildup, and weak growth. Start with recommended doses and observe your plants. Elixir Garden Supplies provides clear guidance on their product labels.
