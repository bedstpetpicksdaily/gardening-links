Buying Guide: 3 Things to Look For

  • NPK Ratio: Check the nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K) percentages on the label. Different plants need different ratios—vegetables typically need higher nitrogen, while flowering plants benefit from more phosphorus.
  • Release Type: Choose between fast-acting water-soluble fertilizers for quick results or slow-release options if you prefer less frequent applications and more consistent long-term nutrition.
  • Plant Type: Select a fertilizer formulated for your specific plants. Specialized formulas for vegetables, flowers, fruit trees, or houseplants will deliver better results than generic all-purpose options.

FAQ About Fertilizers

How often should I fertilize my garden?

Most plants benefit from fertilizing every 2-4 weeks during the growing season, but slow-release fertilizers can last 6-8 weeks per application. Always follow the product instructions, as over-fertilizing can harm plants and damage soil health.

Can I use the same fertilizer for all my plants?

While all-purpose fertilizers work for many plants, specialized formulas deliver better results. Vegetables, flowers, and fruit trees have different nutrient needs, so using targeted fertilizers will maximize growth, blooms, and yields in your garden.
