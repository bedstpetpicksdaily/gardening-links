Frequently Asked Questions

Can I leave the AG-19 on my plants during sunny days?

Yes, and this is one of its best features. The fabric breathes and allows roughly 85% light penetration, so you can leave it in place for multiple days without overheating plants or creating humidity problems. I've left it on for up to four consecutive days during cool spring spells with zero negative effects. Just remove it once daytime temperatures consistently stay above 60°F to avoid leggy growth.

How much does frost protection actually drop the temperature?

AG-19 provides approximately 8 degrees of frost protection under still conditions—meaning if the outside temperature drops to 28°F, the air directly under the blanket stays around 36°F. This works beautifully for light frosts and early-season temperature dips. If you're dealing with a hard freeze or sustained below-freezing temperatures, you'll need additional protection like double-layering or ground heat sources.

Is this the same material used by commercial growers, or is it a consumer knockoff?

Agribon is actually used by commercial operations and home gardeners alike—it's not a simplified consumer version. The AG-19 designation refers to the specific weight and permeability of the fabric. Commercial growers use the same product line but often in larger rolls. You're not paying for a lesser version; you're buying the legitimate material in a practical size for a home garden.

Will this protect against hard freezes in zones 3 or 4?

Single-layer AG-19 provides 8 degrees of protection, which works great for zones 5–8 frost events. If you're in zone 3 or 4 with temperatures regularly dropping below 20°F, plan on dual-layer protection (stacking two blankets) or combining this with other methods like row tunnels or cold frames. Don't count on single-layer coverage if sustained hard freezes are typical in your area.
