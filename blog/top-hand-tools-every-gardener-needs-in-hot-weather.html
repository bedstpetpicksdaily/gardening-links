Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to use hand tools during hot weather?

Early morning (before 10 AM) or late evening (after 6 PM) are ideal times to garden in hot weather. During these cooler periods, your tools will feel more comfortable to use, and your plants will experience less transplant shock or water stress.

Why is ergonomic design important in hot weather gardening?

Ergonomic tools reduce strain and fatigue, allowing you to work efficiently in shorter timeframes. This minimizes heat exposure and prevents injuries that are more common when you're fatigued or working in uncomfortable positions during hot conditions.

How should I maintain Garden Guru Tools in hot, humid weather?

Clean your tools after each use, dry them thoroughly, and store them in a cool, dry location. Apply light oil to metal parts to prevent rust, and check handles regularly for cracks or damage that humidity and heat can accelerate.

Do I need different tools for hot weather versus other seasons?

While you don't need completely different tools, hot-weather gardening benefits from lightweight, ergonomic options that reduce fatigue and allow quick work sessions. Watering equipment becomes more specialized and critical during hot months.
