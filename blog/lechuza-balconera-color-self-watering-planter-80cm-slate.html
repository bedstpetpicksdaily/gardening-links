Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I actually have to water this thing?

Once weekly in normal conditions, twice weekly during extreme heat waves (which July 2026 is guaranteed to bring). The internal water gauge tells you when the reservoir is empty. Most people in temperate zones fill it on Sunday and don't think about it again until Friday. It's not zero maintenance—it's 80% less maintenance than a regular planter.

Does it work with any soil mix?

Yes, but not all soil works equally well. Use quality potting soil (not garden soil). The self-watering system works best when soil has good water retention. If you use gritty, fast-draining soil meant for succulents, the reservoir empties too fast. Standard potting mix with some peat or coco coir works perfectly.

Will this thing crack in winter if I leave it outside?

The material is more cold-resistant than cheap plastic planters, but in freeze-thaw cycles it can develop stress cracks if water sits in the reservoir and freezes. In USDA zones 6 and colder, empty the reservoir in late fall or bring it inside. In warmer zones, it handles winter fine without draining.

Is $10 really the price or is there a catch?

The $10 price is legitimate for the base model through Amazon's deal structure. You're not getting a premium finish or extra features at that price—you're getting solid functionality. There are fancier Lechuza colors and sizes that cost more, but this slate version at $10 is genuine value.
