Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I actually need to refill each model's reservoir?

The 28cm model needs refilling every 2-3 weeks depending on plant type and season; July heat means more frequent refills. The Deltini, with its smaller reservoir, likely requires refilling more often during summer months, though exact capacity isn't published. Both are still dramatically better than daily watering, which is the whole point of the technology.

Can I use regular garden soil or does it need special potting mix?

The 28cm's removable inner pot means you can use whatever potting mix you prefer. For the Deltini, this isn't specified in the available data, but the capillary mat system in both models generally works best with quality potting mix rather than heavy garden soil, which can compact and block water movement.

Will my plant die if I overwater the initial fill?

Self-watering planters actually prevent overwatering because the root system pulls water only as needed through the capillary mat. The 28cm's mechanical indicator tells you exactly when the reservoir is depleted, removing the guesswork. Overwatering the initial fill is less of a concern than with traditional pots.

Does the white high-gloss finish stay clean, or does it show dust and water spots?

Both models feature the same high-gloss finish, which does show water spots and dust more visibly than matte alternatives. Regular light wiping keeps them looking polished, but if your décor is high-traffic or you prefer low-maintenance aesthetics, this finish requires slightly more attention.

Can I use these for outdoor plants or just indoor?

Both work indoors reliably. The 28cm can handle covered outdoor spaces without issue. For fully exposed outdoor use in harsh weather, you'd need to check UV resistance—high-gloss finishes can degrade under intense sun, though short-term covered patio use (spring through early fall) is typically fine.
