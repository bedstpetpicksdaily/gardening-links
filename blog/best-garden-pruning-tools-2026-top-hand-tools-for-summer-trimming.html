Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruners?

Bypass pruners feature two blades that slide past each other, creating a scissor-like cut ideal for fresh, green growth. Anvil pruners have a single blade that closes onto a flat surface, better suited for cutting tougher, dead wood. Bypass pruners typically produce cleaner cuts with less plant damage.

How often should I sharpen my pruning tools?

Sharp tools perform better and require less effort, so it's recommended to sharpen pruning tools every 2-3 months during heavy use, or whenever you notice they're not cutting as cleanly as before. Regular maintenance extends tool life and improves results.

What size branches can hand pruners cut?

Most hand pruners can cleanly cut branches up to ½ inch in diameter. For thicker branches between ½ inch and 1 inch, use loppers. For branches thicker than 1 inch, a pruning saw is your best option.

Are Garden Guru Tools suitable for professional gardeners?

Yes, Garden Guru Tools offers professional-grade equipment alongside beginner-friendly options. Their tools are used by landscapers, horticulturists, and serious gardeners worldwide for their durability, precision, and reliability.

How do I prevent rust on my pruning tools?

Clean your tools after each use, dry them thoroughly, and store them in a dry location. Periodically wipe blades with an oily cloth. Garden Guru Tools uses rust-resistant materials, but regular maintenance extends the life of any tool.
