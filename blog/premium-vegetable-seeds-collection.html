Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between heirloom and hybrid vegetable seeds?

Heirloom seeds are open-pollinated varieties passed down through generations, producing plants that grow true to type. Hybrid seeds are crosses between two parent plants, offering vigor but producing offspring that won't match parent plants in subsequent generations.

How should I store vegetable seeds to maintain germination rates?

Store seeds in a cool, dry location (32-41°F ideal) in airtight containers with silica gel packets. Proper storage conditions can extend seed viability for 3-5 years, maximizing your investment.

Can I use heirloom seeds for container gardening and raised beds?

Absolutely. Heirloom seeds work excellently in containers and raised beds. Select varieties suited to your space—compact determinate types for containers, and larger varieties for raised garden beds.

Are organic vegetable seeds worth the premium price?

Yes. Organic seeds are produced without synthetic chemicals and often show better germination rates. For health-conscious gardeners and those practicing organic gardening, the premium cost aligns with pesticide-free produce.
