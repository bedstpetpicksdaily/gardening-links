Frequently Asked Questions

Can this lopper cut through branches thicker than 1.5 inches?

Not effectively. The 24-inch bypass design is engineered for branches up to roughly 1.5 inches in diameter. Attempting to force thicker wood will either cause jamming or require excessive force that defeats the ergonomic benefits. For thicker branches, you need a pruning saw or pole saw. Knowing this boundary upfront saves frustration.

How often do you need to sharpen or maintain the blades?

With typical residential use (a few hours per week), you're looking at honing the blades every 2-3 seasons. Gardena's steel quality means the edge holds better than budget competitors. Keep the pivot point lubricated with a light machine oil monthly, and clean sap residue with acetone after cutting. Proper maintenance extends the lifespan significantly.

Is the 24-inch length actually better than a 20-inch lopper?

Yes, if you have mature trees or dense shrubs where interior branch access is constant. The extra 4 inches eliminates half the ladder moments. However, the increased length adds leverage that requires slightly more hand strength. For people with limited grip strength or smaller frames, a 20-inch model might feel more comfortable for extended use.

Does the shock absorber system actually reduce hand fatigue?

Measurably, yes. The spring mechanism absorbs impact rather than sending it directly into your hands and wrists. After 45+ minutes of continuous cutting, the difference becomes noticeable compared to basic models. If you have any history of carpal tunnel or wrist sensitivity, this feature justifies the premium over cheaper alternatives.
