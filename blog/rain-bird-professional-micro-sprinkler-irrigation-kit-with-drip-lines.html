Frequently Asked Questions

Will this system work with containers and raised beds, or just in-ground gardens?

Both. The included micro sprinklers adjust to different spray patterns, so you can position them for a 4x8 raised bed, scatter them across container groupings, or even run a line along a vegetable patch. The drip lines are flexible enough to route between pots. Real limitation: the included tubing length is modest (around 50 feet), so if your garden is sprawling, you'll need extension hose. Most people find it covers one main growing area without issue.

How much water does a micro sprinkler actually use compared to hand-watering?

Micro sprinklers use 30-50% less water than overhead watering or soaking with a hose, because water delivers directly to the root zone without evaporating mid-air or running off. In July heat, that's the difference between a water bill that surprises you and one that doesn't. You also avoid the human tendency to either drown plants or forget watering entirely—consistency wins.

Do I need a filter to prevent the micro sprinklers from clogging?

Not mandatory if your tap water is reasonably clean, but recommended if you're pulling from a well, rain barrel, or know your water has sediment. A $3-5 inline filter takes two minutes to install and extends sprinkler life significantly. Rain Bird kits sometimes include one; check your specific listing. Either way, it's cheap insurance against $10 of equipment failing in mid-summer.
