This guide compares three distinct Lechuza self-watering planters side by side: the budget Deltini 8", the mid-range 28cm model, and the premium Cubico and Balconera options. Budget Alternative: Fabric Pot with Saucer & Mulch Layer

The source material mentions fabric pots with saucers and mulch layers as a budget alternative to self-watering planters. This approach uses passive moisture retention—a mulch layer on top of soil slows evaporation and keeps roots consistently hydrated without an active reservoir system.

This is significantly cheaper than any Lechuza model and works well for plants that tolerate variability, but requires more active watering and offers no failsafe mechanism if you're away for weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I actually refill the water reservoir?

It depends on the model and environment. The Deltini (minimal capacity) may need weekly refills in warm, dry conditions. The 28cm and Cubico/Balconera extend to 2–3 weeks between fills in typical indoor or covered patio conditions. Outdoor, sun-exposed planters dry faster regardless of model size.

Is the price difference between Deltini ($10) and Cubico ($40–70) worth it?

Not for raw function. Both use self-watering reservoirs and prevent root rot. The Cubico wins on design, longer refill intervals due to larger capacity, and premium finish. If aesthetics don't matter and you're refilling weekly anyway, the Deltini works fine. If you want to leave for 2+ weeks or need a statement piece, the Cubico justifies the cost.

Will the white high-gloss or Slate finish show dirt and water stains?

Yes, both will. The white high-gloss Deltini shows dust and mineral deposits from tap water. The polished Slate finishes on larger models are designed to look like furniture but will show water stains and require regular wiping. Budget time for maintenance if appearance matters.

Can I use these planters indoors, or are they only for patios?

Both. The Deltini and 28cm are excellent for desks, shelves, and indoor windowsills. The Cubico works indoors and outdoors. The Balconera is larger and more suited to balconies and patios, though it can work indoors if you have the space. No planter is patio-only.

What plants work best in these planters?

Self-watering planters suit plants that prefer consistent moisture: herbs, leafy greens, ferns, and tropical houseplants. Avoid succulents and drought-tolerant plants, which can rot in consistently moist soil. The air-pruning system makes most plants forgiving, but soil moisture tolerance should guide your plant choice.

Do I need special soil, or will regular potting mix work?

The Lechuza system works with standard potting mix. The 28cm and larger models include a removable inner pot, so you can use any soil you prefer. The Deltini is smaller and more restrictive, but standard potting soil is fine. The system handles most soil types; the reservoir does the watering work, not the soil.
