What to Look For

  • Multifunctional tools that do multiple jobs: A good hand cultivator or garden fork can dig, turn soil, and break up clumps without needing separate tools for each task. This saves money and storage space when you're just starting out.

  • Durability over rock-bottom price: The cheapest tools often have weak joints or handles that break after a few uses. Spending $15 instead of $5 on a tool that lasts three seasons is genuinely budget-friendly in the long run.
