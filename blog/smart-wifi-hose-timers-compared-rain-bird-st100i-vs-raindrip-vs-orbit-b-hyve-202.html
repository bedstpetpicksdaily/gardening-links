Frequently Asked Questions

Do these timers work with older WiFi routers?

Yes. The Raindrip was explicitly tested 60 feet from an older router and maintained stable 2.4GHz connections without dropouts. All three models require 2.4GHz WiFi (not 5GHz). If your router is modern, you may need to enable a separate 2.4GHz band in settings.

What happens if my WiFi goes down or the app crashes?

All three models have manual override buttons on the physical device, allowing you to water manually without phone access. Scheduled cycles may be missed during outages, but the devices resume normal operation when WiFi reconnects. Battery-backed backup varies by model.

Can I use these with drip irrigation systems?

Yes. All three models work with both standard hoses and drip irrigation setups. The dual-outlet design is particularly useful here—run drip lines through one zone and standard hoses through the other with independent schedules.

How often do batteries need replacement?

Frequency varies by model and usage pattern. The source material doesn't specify exact intervals, but refers to battery replacement as occasional maintenance. Check the manufacturer specs for your specific model; this typically matters for WiFi backup functionality rather than primary operation.

Does weather-based watering work in areas with spotty weather data?

All three models rely on local weather forecasting to detect rain and cancel cycles. If your area has limited weather data coverage, the feature may work inconsistently. The devices don't have built-in rain sensors—they depend on forecast data, not actual detected precipitation.

Which model integrates with smart home platforms?

The Orbit B-hyve explicitly integrates with Alexa and Google Home. The source material doesn't confirm smart home integration for Rain Bird ST100i or Raindrip, suggesting Orbit is the strongest choice if this is a priority.
