How deep is this really, and what can I actually grow?

The 2-foot depth works beautifully for lettuce, spinach, kale, herbs, peppers, and shallow-rooted flowers. Tomatoes and eggplants squeezed through but need staking. Deep-rooted crops like carrots and potatoes are cramped—they'll grow, but roots coil up instead of extending fully. If deep-rooted vegetables are your main goal, this isn't your bed; step up to a 3-foot model.

Will it tip over or break under the weight of wet soil?

No, the plastic is thick enough to handle the load. Fully saturated soil in a 2x4x2 bed weighs around 350–400 pounds, and the design distributes that pressure evenly across the frame. How many seasons will this last before needing replacement?

Can I use this indoors or on a balcony?

Yes, absolutely. The compact 2x4 footprint works on apartment balconies and patios. Just ensure your balcony can handle the weight—roughly 400 pounds when full and saturated.
