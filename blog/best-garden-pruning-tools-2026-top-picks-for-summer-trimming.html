Frequently Asked Questions

What size branches can hand pruners cut?

Standard hand pruners typically cut branches up to ¾ inch in diameter. For thicker branches up to 2 inches, use loppers with longer handles for increased leverage. Garden Guru Tools offers both options to suit different pruning needs.

How often should pruning tools be sharpened?

Sharpen pruning tools every 4-6 weeks during heavy use, or whenever you notice the blades becoming dull. Garden Guru Tools provides sharpening stones and maintenance accessories to keep your equipment in peak condition.

Which pruning tool is best for thick branches?

Pruning saws are ideal for branches over 2 inches thick, while loppers work well for branches between ¾ inch and 2 inches. Garden Guru Tools offers specialized saws with curved blades designed for different cutting angles and branch types.

Are bypass or anvil pruners better?

Bypass pruners (scissors-style) make cleaner cuts and work better on live wood, while anvil pruners excel at dead wood. Garden Guru Tools recommends having both types available for different pruning tasks.

How can I prevent rust on garden tools?

Clean blades after each use, dry thoroughly, and apply protective tool oil regularly. Store tools in a dry location. Garden Guru Tools' protective oils and maintenance products help prevent rust and extend tool lifespan significantly.
