Starting your first garden can feel overwhelming, but choosing the right raised bed is the foundation for success. What to Look For

  • Appropriate Depth (12-24 inches): Beginners often underestimate how deep soil needs to be. For most vegetables and herbs, 18 inches is the sweet spot. Shallower beds limit root development and dry out quickly, while deeper beds are unnecessarily expensive and heavy to fill with soil.
  • Durable, Non-Toxic Materials: Cedar and composite materials are ideal for beginners because they resist rot, last 10+ years, and won't leach harmful chemicals into your vegetables. Avoid pressure-treated wood from older stock, which can contain arsenic, and untreated pine, which rots within 3-5 years.
  • Easy Assembly and Accessibility: Look for beds with pre-drilled holes, simple corner brackets, or interlocking designs. You'll also want sides low enough (under 36 inches) to reach the center comfortably without stepping into the bed, which compacts soil and harms plants.
