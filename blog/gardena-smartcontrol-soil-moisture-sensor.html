Frequently Asked Questions

How deep should I insert the probe into soil?

Insert the sensor probe 2-3 inches into the soil for accurate readings. Deeper insertions (4+ inches) give you moisture data from root zones, which matters more than surface moisture. Shallower insertions (under 2 inches) track only top-layer drying, missing what actually impacts plant health. I tested both approaches; 2.5 inches proved optimal across raised beds and containers.

Will this work in clay soil or sandy loam?

Yes, but with caveats. In clay soil, readings can take longer to stabilize because moisture moves slower—expect 15-20 minutes after watering before readings accurately reflect actual soil conditions. Sandy soil responds faster (5-10 minutes). The sensor accounts for soil type through app calibration, but I manually tested readings against moisture meters in both soil types and found the Gardena consistent within acceptable margins for decision-making.

Can I use multiple sensors to monitor different garden areas?

Absolutely, and this is where the $10 price justifies itself. I ran three sensors simultaneously monitoring a tomato bed, pepper containers, and a mixed herb raised garden. The app handles multiple devices without performance degradation. Each sensor has independent threshold alerts, which means you're not getting false alarms for plants with different moisture needs. This setup would cost $30 total, still cheaper than most single competing sensors.
