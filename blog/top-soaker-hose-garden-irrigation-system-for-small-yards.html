What to Look For

  • Appropriate Length and Diameter: For small yards, look for soaker hoses between 25-50 feet with a 1/2-inch diameter. This size is manageable to store, easy to route around tight spaces, and delivers consistent pressure without needing excessive water volume. Anything larger and you'll have excess hose cluttering your space; anything smaller and you won't cover enough area efficiently.
  • Drip Emitter Spacing: Choose soaker hoses with emitters spaced every 6-12 inches for small yard beds. This closer spacing prevents dry patches in compact gardens while maintaining water efficiency. With limited space, you want every inch of your soaker hose working hard for your plants.
  • Built-in End Cap and Pressure Regulation: Small yard systems benefit from soaker hoses that include an integrated end cap and pressure regulator. This eliminates the need for additional fittings that take up room and simplifies your setup—critical when you're working with limited space and want a straightforward installation.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How much water does a soaker hose use compared to sprinklers?

    Soaker hoses use 50% less water than sprinklers because water drips slowly directly to soil instead of being sprayed into the air where it evaporates. For small yards, this means lower water bills while keeping plants better hydrated at the root zone.

    What size soaker hose do I need for a small yard?

    For small yards under 500 square feet, a 25-50 foot soaker hose is typically sufficient, depending on your garden layout. If your yard has multiple garden beds, consider a system with multiple hose lines or connectors rather than one long hose to ensure even water distribution.

    How do I set up a soaker hose system on a timer?

    Connect a battery-operated or electric timer to your outdoor faucet, then attach the soaker hose to the timer outlet. Set it to run early morning (6-8 AM) for 30-60 minutes depending on soil type and plant needs—this minimizes evaporation and prevents fungal diseases.

    Can I bury soaker hoses or do they need to stay on top of soil?

    Soaker hoses work best when laid on top of soil and covered with 1-2 inches of mulch, which reduces evaporation and keeps them in place. Burying them can cause clogs from soil particles, though some heavy-duty soaker hoses are designed for burial if you prefer a hidden system.
