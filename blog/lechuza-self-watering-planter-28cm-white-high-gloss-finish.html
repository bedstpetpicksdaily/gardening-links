Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I actually need to refill the reservoir?

It depends on your plant type, light conditions, and season—but expect every 2-3 weeks during average conditions, more frequently in July's heat. Succulents may only need monthly refilling, while moisture-loving plants like ferns need weekly checks. The mechanical gauge removes guesswork, unlike apps or moisture meters.

Does this work for outdoor use or just indoor plants?

Both work, but context matters. Indoors on a shelf, it's ideal. For covered patios, it's fine. Direct outdoor exposure means the white finish will eventually discolor from UV exposure and weather, and heavy rain can interfere with the reservoir system. If you live somewhere with intense July sun, consider covering it or using it in semi-shade.

What if I overwater the initial fill—will my plant die?

Overwatering the reservoir is the #1 mistake owners make. The system is designed for specific water levels; exceeding them forces soil saturation and causes root rot within days. Read the gauge carefully and never fill past the indicator line. If you do overfill by mistake, drain it immediately through the overflow valve.

Can I use regular garden soil or does it need special potting mix?

Garden soil is too dense for this system—it will compact and block water movement through the capillary mat. Use quality potting mix (not topsoil), and consider mixing in perlite for extra drainage if you're growing plants that hate wet roots. Cheapskating on soil defeats the entire purpose of the self-watering design.

Is the white high-gloss finish easy to keep clean?

Surprisingly yes, but with caveats. Water spots appear quickly, and it fingerprints like a touchscreen. A damp microfiber cloth takes seconds to restore shine. In dusty environments or apartments with air conditioning, expect to wipe it weekly if you want the 'high-gloss' effect to actually look premium.
