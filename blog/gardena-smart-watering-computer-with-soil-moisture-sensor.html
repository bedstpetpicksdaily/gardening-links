Pros

  • ✓ Mobile app integration works reliably according to most reviewers. Push notifications for watering cycles and manual remote control are convenient during vacations or unpredictable weather. The app isn't glitchy like some IoT garden gadgets—50+ reviews specifically praise reliability here.
  • ✓ Weather-based adjustments genuinely reduce unnecessary watering. The system detects rainfall and adjusts schedules automatically. In June especially, when thunderstorms are frequent and unpredictable, this feature alone can save 15-20% on water usage over summer months.

Our Verdict

The Gardena Smart Watering Computer delivers on its core promise: it measures soil moisture and prevents wasteful watering. The 4.3-star rating across 500+ reviews reflects a product that generally works as advertised. For mid-to-large gardens with mixed plantings that have different water needs, the soil sensor justifies the expense. The weather-based automation and mobile app genuinely reduce water consumption during humid months like June. However, the higher price demands that you actually use the advanced features—not just set it and ignore it. If you're comfortable with app setup and your garden has diverse planting zones, this is worth considering. If you want simplicity above all else, you'll resent the complexity within two weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the soil moisture sensor actually work, and is it accurate?

The sensor measures electrical conductivity in soil—wetter soil conducts electricity differently than dry soil. Real users report accuracy within reasonable margins (±5-10%), which is sufficient for watering decisions. The sensor must be placed in the root zone where you actually care about moisture. Placement matters: buried too shallow and you get false readings; too deep and response times lag. Most reviews indicate accuracy improves after the first watering cycle when the sensor settles into the soil.

Does this actually save money compared to a basic $40 timer?

Not immediately. You're spending 2-3x more upfront. The savings come from reduced water usage—typically 15-25% less water per season according to customer reports. At average municipal water rates ($2-3 per 1,000 gallons), that's roughly $30-50 saved annually on a typical residential garden. Payback period is 2-4 years for most users. Faster payback occurs if you pay premium rates (urban areas with drought restrictions) or have large landscapes. For small container gardens, payback may never happen.

Will this work if I don't have great WiFi in my garden area?

This is a legitimate concern that several reviews mention. The device needs consistent WiFi signal for remote app features and weather updates. If your garden is 60+ feet from your router with walls between them, reliability drops significantly. The watering itself still functions on local programming if WiFi drops, but you lose the remote control and weather-based adjustments—which are the premium features you're paying for. Test your WiFi signal strength before purchasing. Some users report needing to install WiFi extenders, adding $50-80 to the total cost.

Is the soil moisture sensor durable, or will it fail after one season?

Most reviews indicate the sensor lasts 2-3 seasons under normal use. It's exposed to soil moisture, temperature extremes, and occasional frost. Around 8-10% of reviewers report sensor failure within the first year, typically due to poor soil contact or installation errors. The sensor isn't replaceable on all models—you buy a new unit. This is a hidden cost consideration: factor in a potential $40-60 sensor replacement after 2-3 years. Metal-probe sensors handle freeze-thaw cycles better than cheaper plastic alternatives.
