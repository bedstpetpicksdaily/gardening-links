Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between heirloom and hybrid vegetable seeds?

Heirloom seeds are open-pollinated varieties passed down through generations, producing plants that grow true-to-type. Hybrid seeds are crosses between two different varieties, offering specific traits but won't produce identical plants if saved.

How long do heirloom vegetable seeds stay viable?

Most heirloom seeds remain viable for 3-5 years when stored in cool, dry conditions. Proper storage in airtight containers away from heat and moisture maximizes longevity.

Can I save seeds from heirloom vegetables for next season?

Yes, heirloom varieties are specifically bred for seed saving. Allow plants to fully mature, collect seeds, and store them properly for replanting next season.

Which heirloom vegetables are best for beginners?

Easy heirloom varieties include tomatoes, lettuce, beans, zucchini, and carrots. These have high germination rates and grow well in most climates and garden setups.
