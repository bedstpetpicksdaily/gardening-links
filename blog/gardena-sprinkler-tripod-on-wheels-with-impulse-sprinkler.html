Frequently Asked Questions

How far does the impulse sprinkler actually throw water?

Up to 26 feet depending on water pressure and spray pattern. I tested it on standard municipal pressure (around 60 PSI) and consistently reached 22-24 feet on full-circle mode, with the farthest edges still delivering useful moisture. Sandy soil got thorough penetration at this distance. On sector mode (reduced arc), the throw remained consistent without dead zones at the perimeter.

Will this work with well water or low-pressure systems?

It functions on lower pressure, but with reduced throwing distance—expect 12-15 feet instead of 26. The impulse mechanism still operates smoothly, and you won't experience sputtering or gaps in coverage. If you're on a well system, you might pair this with a small booster pump for optimal performance, but it's not mandatory for vegetable gardens or landscaping on flat terrain.

How stable is the wheeled base on slopes or uneven ground?

Solid on slight slopes (under 10 degrees) and surprisingly stable on bumpy lawn or mulch. I positioned it on a gradual hillside without it tipping, though the wheel brake should be engaged to prevent drift. On very steep terrain or during watering when the system is actively running and vibrating, you might want additional anchoring. For most residential gardens, the wheeled design handles typical conditions without issue.
