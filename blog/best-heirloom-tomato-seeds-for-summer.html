Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best heirloom tomato varieties for hot summer climates?

Heat-tolerant varieties like 'Sungold', 'Cherokee Purple', and 'Brandywine Red' perform well in summer heat, though 'Sungold' is particularly reliable for temperatures above 90°F. For extremely hot regions, consider 'Heatwave II' and 'Phoenix' which are bred specifically to set fruit in intense heat. Plant these varieties in late spring so they mature during your hottest months rather than struggling in peak summer.

How long do heirloom tomatoes take to mature from seed?

Most heirloom tomatoes take 70-85 days from transplant to first harvest, though some slower varieties like 'Cherokee Purple' can take 80-90 days. If you're starting from seed indoors, add 6-8 weeks before transplanting outdoors. To harvest before first frost, count backward from your expected first frost date and start seeds accordingly.

Are heirloom tomato seeds better than hybrid seeds for summer growing?

Heirloom seeds produce fruit with superior flavor and you can save seeds for next year, but hybrids are often more disease-resistant and heat-tolerant. For summer growing specifically, consider hybrid varieties bred for heat tolerance, or choose heat-adapted heirlooms like 'Sungold' if flavor is your priority. Many gardeners use a mix of both for reliable yields and great taste.

Which heirloom tomato seeds produce the earliest harvest?

'Sungold', 'Early Girl', and 'Glacier' are among the fastest-maturing heirlooms, producing fruit in 60-70 days from transplant. 'Sungold' is particularly praised for consistent early production even in cool summers, while 'Glacier' is ideal for short-season gardeners. Plant these in early spring to get harvests well before late-summer heat stress reduces yields.
