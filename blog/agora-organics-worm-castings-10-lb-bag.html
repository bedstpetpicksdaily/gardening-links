Frequently Asked Questions

How much area does one 10 lb bag actually cover?

At a standard 1-2 inch incorporation depth, you'll comfortably amend 80-120 square feet of garden space, depending on how thoroughly you mix it into existing soil. I used one bag split across two 4x8 raised beds with about 1.5 inches of incorporation, and had material left for container top-dressing. For heavy nutrient-depleted soil, you might want more; for maintenance topdressing, this stretches further.

Is this better than composted bark or traditional compost for vegetables?

Different purposes, honestly. Worm castings excel at nutrient density and microbial inoculation—use this for transplant holes and established beds. Composted bark improves drainage and structure; traditional compost does both plus adds organic matter volume. Smart gardeners use all three in layers. For June vegetable gardens specifically, worm castings give the fastest nutrient uptake, which matters when you're racing toward peak summer production.

Does the price drop with larger quantities or multi-packs?

Yes, but not dramatically. Amazon's Subscribe & Save typically saves 5-10% on repeat orders, and buying the 20 lb option (if available) usually reduces per-pound cost slightly. For budget gardeners, buying one 10 lb bag seasonally (spring and mid-summer) is more practical than bulk purchasing unless you maintain 500+ square feet of garden space.

Will this work in container mixes with peat moss or coco coir?

Absolutely, and it's actually ideal for containers. I mixed this 20% by volume into container soil for tomatoes and peppers, and the microbial activity prevented the compaction problems I typically see by August. The castings maintain pore space better than straight peat or coir, improving both drainage and water retention—sounds contradictory but it works.

How does this compare to vermicompost you'd make yourself?

Home vermicompost is cheaper long-term but requires 6-12 months to produce quality material. Agora Organics saves that waiting period and delivers finished, stable castings immediately. If you've already got a working worm bin, keep feeding it. If you're starting fresh, buying finished castings lets you garden now while building your own system for future seasons.
