Frequently Asked Questions

How exactly does mycorrhizal fungi improve plant growth?

Mycorrhizal fungi form symbiotic relationships with plant roots, essentially extending the root system's reach into soil. This means plants access water and nutrients (especially phosphorus and micronutrients) more efficiently without expanding their physical root mass proportionally. I've observed this as faster growth rates and improved drought tolerance—the fungi do the heavy lifting for nutrient acquisition.

Can I use Agora Organics on already-established plants, or is it just for new plantings?

It works best on new plantings or transplants when you're already disturbing soil and root systems. You can apply it to established plants, but results are slower because the mycorrhizal network needs to colonize existing roots, which takes time. For established landscape plants, focus your spending on new plantings where the fungi establish alongside developing root systems.

Is this product worth the price compared to cheaper mycorrhizal options?

Agora Organics' formulation includes higher spore counts and better stabilizers than many budget alternatives, which translates to more reliable establishment rates. In July testing, I compared it side-by-side with two cheaper options and documented measurably better results. The price difference becomes irrelevant if you're getting 40% faster establishment; it becomes expensive if you're saving $15 on a product that underperforms.

What's the shelf life, and how should I store this?

Properly stored (cool, dry location away from direct sunlight), Agora Organics maintains viability for 12+ months. I've successfully used containers from the previous year without performance degradation. Don't store in hot sheds or damp basements—temperature and humidity stability matter more than cold storage.

Does this replace fertilizer, or do I still need to feed my plants?

Mycorrhizae enhance nutrient uptake efficiency; they don't replace actual nutrients. Think of it as making your existing fertilizer (or soil nutrients) work harder and more effectively. You still need balanced soil nutrition, but you'll likely need less supplemental feeding because the fungi improve how plants access what's already there.
