Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to water a garden during hot weather?

Water during early morning (5-8 AM) or late evening (6-9 PM) when temperatures are cooler and evaporation rates are lowest. This ensures more water reaches plant roots rather than evaporating from soil and foliage.

How often should I water my garden in extreme heat?

Most plants need daily watering in extreme heat, while heat-tolerant species may need water every 2-3 days. Check soil moisture 2-3 inches deep; if it feels dry, it's time to water. Using Hoselink's timer-equipped systems makes consistent watering effortless.

Why should I use a retractable hose reel instead of a traditional hose?

Retractable hose reels like Hoselink's protect hoses from UV damage, prevent kinks that reduce flow, eliminate tangles, and provide convenient storage. In hot weather, this protection significantly extends hose lifespan.

How much water does a garden typically need in hot weather?

Most gardens need 1-2 inches of water weekly in hot weather, delivered slowly to allow soil absorption. Deep, infrequent watering encourages deeper root growth, making plants more drought-resistant than shallow daily watering.
