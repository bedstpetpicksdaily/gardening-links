What to Look For

1. Cutting Power and Blade Quality

Look for shears with a cutting diameter of at least 20mm (3/4 inch) and premium blade materials like titanium-coated steel or Japanese stainless steel. The motor should deliver consistent cutting force—check reviews for performance on woody stems, not just soft herbaceous plants. Higher wattage (typically 7.2V-20V) generally means more cutting power and cleaner cuts with less jamming.

2. Battery Runtime and Charging Speed

For most home gardeners, 45-60 minutes of runtime is sufficient for typical pruning sessions. However, if you have a large property, aim for 75+ minutes. Charging time should be under 2 hours; anything longer becomes frustrating during peak season. Check whether the battery is compatible with other tools you own—this can save hundreds of dollars if you already have a brand ecosystem.

3. Ergonomics and Weight

You'll be holding these shears overhead and at awkward angles for extended periods. Weight should ideally be under 4 pounds (lighter is better for overhead work). Grip design matters tremendously—rubberized, ergonomic handles reduce hand fatigue. Look for adjustable cutting widths or thumb switches that prevent accidental activation, and consider vibration dampening features if you're sensitive to tool vibration.

4. Safety Features and Build Quality

Essential safety features include automatic shutoff (stops after 20 minutes of inactivity), two-handed trigger operation, and a protective blade guard. Check the overall build quality—reinforced plastic or aluminum bodies outlast cheaper alternatives. Warranty length (typically 1-3 years) indicates manufacturer confidence in durability. Water resistance is a bonus if you'll be using them in morning dew or after light rain.
