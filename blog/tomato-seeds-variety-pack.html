Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to start tomato seeds indoors?

Start tomato seeds 6-8 weeks before your last spring frost date. This gives seedlings enough time to develop strong roots before transplanting into garden beds or raised planters.

How do I store tomato seeds to maintain viability?

Store seeds in a cool, dry place (50-60°F) in airtight containers. Properly stored heirloom tomato seeds can remain viable for 4-6 years.

Can I save seeds from heirloom tomato plants?

Yes, heirloom and open-pollinated varieties are perfect for seed saving. Allow tomatoes to fully ripen on the plant, extract seeds, ferment them for 2-3 days, then dry completely before storage.

What's the difference between heirloom and hybrid tomato seeds?

Heirloom seeds produce plants identical to parents and can be saved for next season. Hybrid seeds offer disease resistance and consistency but don't produce viable seeds for replanting.
