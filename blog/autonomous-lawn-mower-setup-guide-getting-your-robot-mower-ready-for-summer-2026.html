Frequently Asked Questions

How long does setup actually take for an autonomous lawn mower?

Initial setup typically requires 2–4 hours, with the boundary wire installation consuming the majority of time. This involves outlining your entire lawn perimeter with chalk, laying wire with stakes every few feet, and securing wire connectors. Charging dock installation and mobile app configuration usually take 30–60 minutes combined. First-time users should plan for the longer estimate.

Can autonomous mowers handle hills and uneven terrain?

Capability varies by model. The ANTHBOT system can manage some slope, but steep hills (over 20° gradient) or highly uneven ground may reduce performance, cause navigation errors, or prevent the mower from returning to the charging dock. Terrain mapping before purchase is essential if your lawn has significant elevation changes.

What maintenance is required during summer?

Regular maintenance includes checking and sharpening cutting blades every 2–3 weeks during peak growing season, inspecting boundary wires for damage after storms, clearing debris from the charging dock contacts, and checking sensor cleanliness. Battery performance should also be monitored if temperatures exceed 85°F, as heat can reduce charge capacity.

How long does the battery typically last on a full charge?

This varies by lawn size and cutting mode. Most autonomous mowers operate for 60–120 minutes per charge, allowing them to cover 0.5–1.5 acres before returning to the dock to recharge. Scheduling multiple shorter cuts throughout the day is more effective than attempting one long session.

Is the system waterproof and safe in rain?

ANTHBOT mowers include rain sensors that automatically pause operation during wet weather, protecting both the equipment and your lawn (cutting wet grass causes clumping and turf damage). The mower itself is weather-resistant, but prolonged water exposure should be avoided. Always allow the lawn to dry before resuming automatic scheduling after rain.
