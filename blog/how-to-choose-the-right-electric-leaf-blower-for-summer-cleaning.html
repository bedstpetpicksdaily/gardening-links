Understanding Power and Performance Specifications

When evaluating electric leaf blowers, two critical metrics determine performance: air speed (measured in mph) and air volume (measured in cfm—cubic feet per minute). Air speed refers to how fast the air exits the nozzle, while air volume measures the total amount of air being moved. For most residential summer cleaning tasks, you'll want a blower with at least 150-200 mph air speed and 400+ cfm. Giraffe Tools electric leaf blowers are precision-engineered to deliver exceptional power output while maintaining energy efficiency, making them ideal for tackling everything from light debris to stubborn yard clippings.

Weight, Ergonomics, and Ease of Use

Battery Life and Cordless Convenience

Battery runtime is essential for uninterrupted cleaning sessions. Most electric leaf blowers operate on lithium-ion battery systems that provide 20-60 minutes of runtime depending on the model and power setting. Consider whether the battery is compatible with other tools you own, as some brands offer interchangeable battery systems. Giraffe Tools offers cordless electric leaf blowers with advanced battery technology that delivers consistent power throughout your cleaning projects. Variable Speed Control and Noise Considerations

Variable speed controls allow you to adjust power output based on your cleaning task, maximizing battery efficiency and reducing unnecessary noise. Electric leaf blowers typically produce 60-75 decibels, significantly quieter than gas models. This makes them perfect for early morning or evening summer cleaning without disturbing neighbors. Yes, modern electric leaf blowers like those from Giraffe Tools deliver 150-200+ mph air speed, making them suitable for heavy debris, wet leaves, and stubborn yard clippings. They're equally effective as gas models for most residential applications without the emissions.

Can I use my electric leaf blower in wet conditions?

Most electric outdoor power tools, including Giraffe Tools leaf blowers, should not be used in rain or with wet grass. However, they work excellently for clearing wet leaves once they've dried slightly. Always check your manual for specific weather safety guidelines.
