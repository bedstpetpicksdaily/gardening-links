Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I water my garden during summer?

Most gardens need deep watering 2-3 times per week during summer, but this depends on soil type, plant selection, and local temperature. Use a moisture meter from Garden Guru Tools to check soil hydration and adjust watering frequency accordingly.

What's the best time to water gardens in summer?

Water early morning (before 9 AM) or late evening (after 6 PM) to minimize evaporation. Midday watering wastes water as heat causes evaporation before plants can absorb moisture.

Why is a soaker hose better than a sprinkler for summer?

Soaker hoses deliver water directly to soil, reducing evaporation loss by up to 50% compared to sprinklers. This conserves water, saves money, and delivers more water where plants actually need it during hot weather.

Can I use regular tools for summer gardening?

While you can, professional-grade tools from Garden Guru Tools are designed to withstand heat, provide better ergonomics, and deliver superior performance. Quality tools make summer gardening easier and more efficient.

How does mulch help with summer watering?

Mulch acts as insulation, reducing soil temperature and preventing water evaporation. A 2-3 inch layer of mulch can reduce watering needs by 25-50%, making it essential for summer gardening efficiency.
