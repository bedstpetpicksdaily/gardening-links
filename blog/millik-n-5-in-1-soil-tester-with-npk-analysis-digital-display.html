Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the NPK analysis compared to professional lab testing?

The Millikën gives you directional accuracy, not lab accuracy. It'll tell you 'this bed is nitrogen-deficient' reliably, but the exact PPM reading might be off by 10-20%. For casual gardeners and vegetable growers, that's sufficient. For precision nutrient management, send samples to a real lab. Think of this as a screening tool, not a final diagnosis.

Can you use this on potted plants and raised beds, or just in-ground soil?

Both work fine. The probe is long enough for 12-inch deep pots and raised beds. The main consideration: make sure the soil is moist enough for the probe to insert without force. Bone-dry, compacted soil will give you false readings because the sensors can't make proper contact. Water your test area lightly 30 minutes before checking.

Is the digital display durable, or does it fail quickly like other budget testers?

The display itself is durable—LCD screens are reliable. The failure point most users hit is actually the probe connectors corroding after 1-2 seasons of repeated moisture exposure. Store it indoors and dry the probe after use. Users who do this report 2-3 years of reliable performance; those who leave it sitting in the garden fail faster.
