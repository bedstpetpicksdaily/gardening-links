Understanding Your Pruning Tool Options

The pruning tool landscape can seem overwhelming with so many options available. Garden Guru Tools simplifies this by offering several key categories of pruning implements, each designed for specific tasks. Hand pruners (secateurs) are ideal for cutting branches up to half an inch thick, making them perfect for deadheading flowers and trimming small branches. Bypass secateurs use a scissor-like cutting action and work best on living wood, while anvil secateurs are better suited for dead or hardwood.

Loppers extend your reach and cutting power, allowing you to tackle branches up to two inches in diameter without straining your hands and wrists. Garden Guru's lopper collection features both bypass and anvil options with extended handles for maximum leverage. For thicker branches and tougher jobs, pruning saws deliver the cutting power you need with minimal effort, making them indispensable for larger landscaping projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruning tools?

Bypass tools use a scissor-like cutting action and work best on living wood, providing clean cuts. Anvil tools have a flat surface and cutting blade, making them better for dead or hardened branches. Garden Guru offers both types to suit your specific needs.

How do I maintain my Garden Guru pruning tools?

Keep blades clean and dry after each use, wipe away sap with a cloth, and occasionally sharpen with a whetstone. Garden Guru provides maintenance accessories and replacement parts to keep your tools performing optimally.

What size branches can hand pruners cut?

Hand pruners (secateurs) typically cut branches up to half an inch in diameter. For thicker branches, upgrade to Garden Guru loppers, which handle branches up to two inches thick with ease.
