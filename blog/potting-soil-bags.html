Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between potting soil and garden soil?

Potting soil is lighter and more porous than garden soil, designed specifically for containers to ensure proper drainage and prevent root rot. Garden soil is heavier and better suited for in-ground planting.

How much potting soil do I need for my containers?

Calculate based on your container size. A 10-quart bag typically fills a 12-14 inch diameter pot. Measure your container's depth and width to determine quantity needed.

Can I reuse potting soil from previous seasons?

Yes, but refresh it by mixing in fresh potting soil (50/50 blend) and adding compost or slow-release fertilizer to replenish nutrients.

Which potting soil is best for vegetables?

Look for potting soils with added perlite or vermiculite for drainage, and those enriched with fertilizers or organic matter to support vegetable growth.
