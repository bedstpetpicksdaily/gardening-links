Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Agora actually hold 300 feet without tangling or kinking?

Yes, but only if you feed it deliberately during storage. The guide system works well, but careless winding (rolling it up in a rush) will still create kinks in the outer wraps. Think of it like a boat winch—the mechanism is solid, but user technique matters. Most reviewers report success when they treat it as a 'proper tool' rather than 'just throw the hose on.'

Is this better than a wall-mounted or stationary reel for a budget gardener?

Not necessarily. A $25 wall-mounted reel works perfectly fine if your hose stays within 6 feet of the spigot. The Agora's advantage is portability—you can roll it to different garden zones or store it away quickly. If you have multiple watering areas or an irregularly shaped yard, the mobility adds real value. Otherwise, you're paying for convenience you won't use.

How durable is this through multiple seasons? Will the wheels fail?

The pneumatic tires are the durability wildcard. They're better than plastic wheels but still subject to UV degradation and punctures. Most users report 2-3 seasons of heavy use before flat tires or visible wear appear. Replacement tires are available for $15-20, making repair feasible. Compare this to a $50 all-plastic cart that might fail entirely after one harsh winter—the durability math can still favor the Agora if you maintain it.
