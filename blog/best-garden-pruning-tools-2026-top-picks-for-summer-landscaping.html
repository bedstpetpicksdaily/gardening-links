Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruners?

Bypass pruners work like scissors with two curved blades that pass each other, making clean cuts ideal for live, green stems. Anvil pruners have one sharp blade that presses against a flat surface, better for dead wood and tougher branches. Garden Guru Tools offers both types for different applications.

How often should I sharpen my pruning tools?

Sharp pruning tools should be honed weekly during heavy use and professionally sharpened 1-2 times per season. Dull blades crush plant tissue and increase disease risk. Garden Guru Tools provides replacement blade kits and sharpening service information.

Can I use the same pruning tool for all plant types?

Different plants require different approaches. Hand pruners work for most flowers and small branches, but hedges benefit from hedge shears, and thick branches need loppers or saws. Garden Guru Tools' starter kits include versatile options suitable for most garden situations.

What's the ideal blade length for hand pruners?

Hand pruners typically range from 6-8 inches. Smaller hands benefit from 6-inch models, while larger hands may prefer 8-inch pruners for better leverage. Garden Guru Tools offers multiple sizes to ensure comfortable fit regardless of hand size.

Are bypass pruners better than anvil for general gardening?

For most home gardeners, bypass pruners are preferable because they make cleaner cuts on living plants without crushing tissue. Anvil pruners excel with dead wood. Garden Guru Tools' bypass collections are ideal for typical summer landscape maintenance.
