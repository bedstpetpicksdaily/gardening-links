Frequently Asked Questions

What is Garden Grove evacuation and why do I need it?

Garden Grove evacuation refers to the process of removing water, air pockets, and debris from garden irrigation systems and soil before winter or extended periods of non-use. This prevents freeze damage to pipes, promotes better water absorption, and removes trapped contaminants that can clog your system.

When should I evacuate my Garden Grove irrigation system?

You should evacuate your system in fall before the first frost, typically between October and November depending on your climate zone. If you're closing down a seasonal garden or preparing for storage, evacuation should be done immediately before shutdown to prevent ice formation and pipe damage.

How do I evacuate my Garden Grove system myself?

Turn off the water supply, open all drain valves and low-point drains to release water completely, then use compressed air at 50-80 PSI to blow out remaining water from all lines and emitters. Never exceed 80 PSI as this can damage your system components.

Can I use my Garden Grove system immediately after evacuation?

No, you should wait until spring or when temperatures consistently stay above freezing before reactivating your system. Close all drain valves, turn the water supply back on gradually, and check all connections for leaks before resuming regular irrigation.
