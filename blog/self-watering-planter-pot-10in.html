Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the water reservoir last in a 10-inch self-watering planter?

Most 10-inch self-watering planters can sustain plants for 7-14 days depending on plant type, soil moisture, and climate conditions. Check the water level weekly for best results.

Can I use a self-watering planter for succulents and cacti?

Self-watering planters aren't ideal for succulents as they prefer dry conditions. Use standard pots with drainage holes for drought-tolerant plants instead.

Is a 10-inch planter suitable for outdoor use?

Yes, most quality self-watering planters work well outdoors. Choose UV-resistant materials and ensure proper drainage to prevent water accumulation during heavy rains.

How do I refill the water reservoir in my self-watering pot?

Most models have a fill tube or opening at the top. Simply pour water into the designated opening until full, and the wicking system distributes it to plant roots automatically.
