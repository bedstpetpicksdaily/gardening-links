Understanding PSI and GPM: The Core Specifications

PSI (pounds per square inch) and GPM (gallons per minute) are the two most critical specifications when choosing an electric pressure washer. PSI measures the force of the water spray, while GPM determines how much water flows through the nozzle per minute. For deck and patio cleaning, you typically want between 1,300-2,000 PSI, which is powerful enough to remove dirt and algae without damaging wood or composite materials. The ideal GPM range is 1.5-2.0 for residential applications.

Giraffe Tools electric pressure washers are engineered with optimal PSI and GPM combinations, ensuring you get enough cleaning power for various surfaces without risk of damage. Frequently Asked Questions

What PSI do I need to clean a wooden deck safely?

For wooden decks, use a pressure washer with 1,300-1,500 PSI maximum. Anything higher risks damaging the wood fibers. Composite decks can typically handle 1,500-2,000 PSI. Always test on an inconspicuous area first and use wide-angle nozzles (25-40 degrees) to distribute pressure evenly.

Can I use an electric pressure washer in the rain?

It's not recommended to use electric pressure washers in heavy rain or thunderstorms due to electrical safety risks. Light drizzle is generally acceptable, but always ensure your outdoor outlets have GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter) protection for added safety.

How often should I clean my deck and patio?

Most experts recommend cleaning your deck and patio at least twice yearly—once in spring and again in fall. If you live in a humid climate with heavy algae growth, quarterly cleaning may be necessary. Regular maintenance prevents dirt and mold buildup that becomes harder to remove over time.
