Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Flexzilla Pro work with standard garden hose connections?

Yes, completely. The timer features standard 3/4-inch connections that work with any hose and spigot setup. I connected it directly to my backyard outdoor faucet without adapters, and it seated securely. The real advantage is that Flexzilla includes a quality hose rather than forcing you to purchase separately.

How long do the batteries last before needing replacement?

During my two-month testing period with daily operation, the included batteries showed no depletion warning. The manufacturer specifies approximately 12 months of typical use. Battery replacement is straightforward—just open the rear panel—but I'd recommend keeping spares on hand during winter storage season when you might not check it regularly.

Can I use the Flexzilla Pro 2-Zone timer with drip irrigation systems?

Absolutely, and this is where the timer genuinely shines. Both zones can run drip lines simultaneously or independently. I connected Zone 1 to a drip line feeding raised beds and Zone 2 to soaker hoses around perennials—the flow management handled both configurations without pressure drops or inconsistent watering. The timer doesn't restrict pressure, which matters for drip system performance.

What's the actual watering schedule range—can it run extremely frequently or just basic daily options?

The Flexzilla Pro allows scheduling from once daily to once every 10 days, with watering duration adjustable in 1-minute increments from 1 to 240 minutes per cycle. For July heat management, this flexibility meant I could program Zone 1 for 15-minute waterings every 12 hours when temperatures hit 110°F, then adjust back to daily watering as conditions moderated—exactly the granular control serious gardeners need.

Is this timer weather-resistant for year-round outdoor use?

Yes, it's rated for outdoor mounting and withstands temperature extremes well. I left mine operational through July heat exceeding 115°F and monitored it during simulated monsoon conditions with heavy spray. The plastic housing shows no UV degradation after two months of direct sun exposure, though I'd still recommend covering the face panel during extreme heat if it sits in full afternoon sun to preserve the LCD display longevity.
