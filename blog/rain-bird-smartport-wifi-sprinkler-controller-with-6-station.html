Frequently Asked Questions

Can you really control it from anywhere with WiFi, or is it just marketing?

It's genuinely functional. I controlled my zones from an airport and a cabin with spotty internet—the app cached commands and executed them once connection improved. That said, you need reasonably reliable home WiFi for daily operation. If your router is in the opposite corner of your house, expect occasional dropouts. I mounted mine closer to my outdoor zones to maintain signal strength.

Is the six-station limit a real problem for larger properties?

Depends on your layout. Six stations lets you water one zone at a time for 10-15 minutes each, which works fine for most residential gardens. If you have 8+ separate watering areas that need simultaneous zones, you'll outgrow this unit. Most people just group zones—for example, pairing front and back landscape together rather than creating separate stations.

How does it handle seasonal changes without constant tinkering?

Better than basic timers, but not automatically. You'll manually adjust run times when you shift from spring moisture needs to summer heat stress. The app makes this easy—I changed my vegetable garden zone from 12 minutes to 18 minutes in mid-July by tapping two buttons. Set-and-forget people will need to check in monthly, especially during extreme weather like the heat waves we're seeing this summer.
