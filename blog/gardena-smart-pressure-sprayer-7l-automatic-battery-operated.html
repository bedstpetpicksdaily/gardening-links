Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the battery actually last per charge?

Real-world runtime clusters around 60-75 minutes of continuous spraying at moderate pressure settings. This varies based on spray pattern intensity—mist mode extends runtime, while maximum pressure stream depletes battery faster. For most gardens, one charge handles morning or evening watering without interruption.

Will this work for both delicate flowers and tough shrubs?

Yes. The adjustable nozzle genuinely transitions from fine mist suitable for seedlings and hanging baskets to a concentrated stream powerful enough for shrub washing and soil settling. The dial mechanism requires minimal effort to switch patterns, which matters when you're moving between different planting zones.

What do owners complain about most?

The primary complaint centers on seal degradation after 18-24 months of regular use, resulting in slow leaks around the pump base. The secondary issue is the app functionality—it's poorly designed and disconnects frequently, so most experienced users disable it entirely. Neither issue prevents the sprayer from functioning, but both affect long-term satisfaction.

Is the 7-liter size manageable to hold and spray?

When filled completely, 7 liters weighs roughly 15-16 pounds, which is noticeably heavy but manageable for 10-15 minute spraying sessions. If you have wrist or shoulder issues, consider emptying it to 5 liters or testing the weight in-store before committing. Most able-bodied adults handle it without significant fatigue, especially compared to manual pump sprayers that demand repetitive arm strength.

How does this compare to the Ryobi One+ system?

For standalone spraying functionality and ease of use, the Gardena performs nearly identically at lower price points. Choose Ryobi only if battery compatibility with other tools matters to your workflow.
