Frequently Asked Questions

Is Roundup safe to use around pets and children in Florida?

Roundup with glyphosate is generally safe when used as directed, but you should keep pets and children away from treated areas until the spray dries completely, typically 30 minutes to a few hours depending on weather. Always read the label instructions carefully and consider using pet-safe alternatives if you have animals that frequent your yard frequently.

Does Roundup Pump N Go work on Florida weeds like crabgrass and St. Augustine grass?

Roundup works best on annual weeds and is most effective on actively growing plants, but it may not eliminate crabgrass completely or regrow St. Augustine grass if you're trying to kill it selectively. For Florida-specific weed problems, you may need a selective herbicide designed for your grass type rather than a non-selective option like Roundup.

What's the best time of year to spray Roundup in Florida?

Spring and fall are ideal for herbicide application in Florida when weeds are actively growing and temperatures are between 60-85°F, avoiding the intense summer heat that can reduce effectiveness. Avoid spraying during Florida's rainy season (June-September) as rain can wash away the herbicide before it's absorbed by the plants.

How long does the Pump N Go 2 sprayer last and is it refillable?

The Pump N Go 2 is a ready-to-use, pressurized sprayer that typically contains enough solution to cover 1,500-5,000 square feet depending on weed density, and it is not refillable once empty. You'll need to purchase a new sprayer for your next application, making it convenient but potentially more expensive for large properties requiring frequent treatments.
