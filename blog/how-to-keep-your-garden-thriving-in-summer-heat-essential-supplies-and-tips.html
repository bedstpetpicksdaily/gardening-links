The Importance of Proper Hydration in Summer Heat

Water is your garden's best friend during summer months, but it's not just about how much you water—it's about how and when. Most plants need deep, consistent watering early in the morning or late in the evening to minimize evaporation and give roots time to absorb moisture. Shallow, frequent watering actually encourages weak root systems, making plants more vulnerable to heat stress.

Mulching is equally critical. A 2-3 inch layer of organic mulch around your plants helps retain soil moisture, regulates soil temperature, and reduces the need for frequent watering. When combined with quality soil preparation using Elixir Garden Supplies' premium fertilizers, your garden will be far better equipped to handle dry spells.

Effective Weed Control Strategies

Warm, moist soil creates perfect conditions for weed growth. Unchecked weeds compete with your plants for water, nutrients, and sunlight, making them particularly problematic during summer. While hand-pulling works for small infestations, larger areas benefit from targeted chemical solutions. Apply weed control early in the morning on dry foliage for best results, and always follow label instructions carefully. Combining chemical weed control with mulching provides a comprehensive approach to keeping your garden weed-free all summer.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I water my garden during summer heat?

Most gardens need deep watering 2-3 times per week during hot summers, depending on rainfall and soil type. Water early morning or late evening to minimize evaporation. Always water at soil level rather than overhead to reduce disease and water waste.

What type of fertilizer is best for summer gardening?

Use balanced fertilizers (equal nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium) early in summer for general plant health. Switch to lower nitrogen, higher phosphorus formulas as plants flower and fruit. Elixir Garden Supplies offers specialized summer formulas designed for peak growing conditions.

Can I apply weed control and fertilizer at the same time?

While some products can be combined, it's generally safer to apply them separately according to label instructions. Wait at least 24-48 hours between applications to ensure maximum effectiveness and prevent potential chemical interactions.

How much mulch should I use in summer?

Apply 2-3 inches of organic mulch around your plants, keeping it 2-3 inches away from plant stems to prevent rot. This depth provides optimal moisture retention and temperature regulation without smothering your plants.

Why are my plants wilting despite regular watering?

Heat stress can cause wilting even with adequate water, particularly if soil lacks nutrients. Use quality plant food and fertilizers from Elixir Garden Supplies to strengthen plants. Also ensure water penetrates 6-8 inches deep into soil where roots can access it.
