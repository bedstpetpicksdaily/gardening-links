Frequently Asked Questions

Does the 14-inch terracotta pot have drainage holes?

Yes, terracotta planter pots include pre-drilled drainage holes at the bottom to prevent water accumulation and root rot. Always use a saucer or drainage base when placing indoors.

What plants grow best in a 14-inch terracotta planter?

This size works well for tomatoes, peppers, herbs, flowering plants, and small shrubs. It's versatile enough for both vegetables and ornamental plants in containers.

How often should I water plants in terracotta pots?

Terracotta is porous and evaporates water quickly. Check soil moisture every 2-3 days and water when the top inch feels dry, especially in warm weather.

Can I use a 14-inch terracotta pot indoors?

Yes, but place a protective saucer underneath to catch water drainage and protect your floors. Ensure adequate light and proper drainage for indoor plant health.

Is terracotta frost-resistant?

Standard terracotta can crack in freezing temperatures. For winter use, empty the pot, allow it to dry completely, or bring it indoors to extend its lifespan.
