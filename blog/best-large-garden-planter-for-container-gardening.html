If you're looking to maximize your gardening space without committing to in-ground beds, large container planters are your secret weapon. Whether you're working with a small patio, balcony, or just want the flexibility to rearrange your garden layout, choosing the right large planter can make the difference between a thriving garden and a frustrating experience.

What to Look For

  • Drainage capability: Large planters need multiple drainage holes (at least 2-3 for a 20+ gallon container) to prevent root rot and waterlogging. Look for designs where drainage holes are positioned low enough that water doesn't pool at the bottom, and consider whether the planter sits on feet or a raised base.
  • Material durability and weight: For large planters, you'll want something that's weather-resistant but not so heavy that you can't move it when needed. Lightweight options like quality resin or fabric offer the best of both worlds—they withstand years of sun exposure without cracking, while remaining manageable for one or two people to reposition.
  • Size that matches your plants: The general rule is one gallon of soil per one inch of mature plant height. For vegetables, herbs, and flowering plants, aim for containers that are at least 18-24 inches deep and 18 inches wide. Anything smaller will require constant watering and limit root development.

What to Avoid

  • Ceramic or terracotta large planters: While beautiful, ceramic and terracotta are porous materials that dry out extremely quickly in large containers—especially in warm climates.
