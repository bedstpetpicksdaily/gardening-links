Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I replace my garden gloves?

High-quality garden gloves typically last 1-2 seasons with regular use. Replace them sooner if you notice tears, thinning material, or reduced grip performance. Keep spare pairs on hand so you can rotate them and extend their lifespan.

Can I wash garden gloves in the washing machine?

Most garden gloves can be hand-washed with mild soap and water, but avoid the washing machine as it may damage reinforced areas and reduce durability. Air dry completely before storing to prevent mold and odor.

Are waterproof garden gloves really necessary?

For gardeners managing irrigation systems or working with wet soil regularly, waterproof gloves prevent moisture from causing discomfort and extending dry time. However, breathable non-waterproof gloves work fine for typical gardening tasks if you don't mind occasional moisture.

What's the difference between gardening gloves and work gloves?

Garden gloves are specifically designed with better dexterity, thorn resistance, and moisture management for planting, seed-starting, and plant care. Work gloves prioritize heavy-duty protection but sacrifice the precision grip needed for delicate gardening tasks.

How do I choose the right size garden gloves?

Measure around your hand at the widest point (excluding the thumb) and compare to the manufacturer's size chart. Gloves should fit snugly without restricting movement; too-loose gloves reduce control and too-tight ones cause hand fatigue and blisters.
