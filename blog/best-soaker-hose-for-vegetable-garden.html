Frequently Asked Questions

What length soaker hose do I need for my vegetable garden?

Most vegetable gardens benefit from soaker hoses between 25-100 feet, depending on your garden's layout and water pressure. A good rule of thumb is to measure your garden's perimeter or the linear footage of your planting rows, then add 10-15 feet for connections and overlaps. For long gardens over 75 feet, consider using multiple hoses or a branching system to maintain consistent water pressure.

How often should I water my vegetable garden with a soaker hose?

Most vegetables need 1-2 inches of water per week, which typically requires running your soaker hose for 30-60 minutes depending on water pressure and soil type. Water early in the morning to reduce disease and evaporation, and adjust frequency based on rainfall, temperature, and your soil's water retention—sandy soil needs more frequent watering than clay.

Is drip irrigation or soaker hose better for vegetables?

Soaker hoses are more affordable and easier to install for most home gardeners, while drip irrigation systems with emitters offer more precise water control and efficiency. For vegetable gardens, soaker hoses work well for rows and beds, but drip systems excel if you have irregularly spaced plants or want to reduce water waste by up to 50%.

What PSI and water pressure do I need for a soaker hose?

Most soaker hoses work best with 20-40 PSI of water pressure, though they can function at lower pressures. Check your home's water pressure with a gauge, and if it exceeds 60 PSI, install a pressure regulator to prevent the hose from bursting and to ensure even water distribution along the entire length.
