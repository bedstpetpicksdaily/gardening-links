Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Suncast resin compare to wood raised beds in terms of actual lifespan?

Wood (untreated) typically lasts 3-7 years before rot sets in depending on climate and moisture exposure. This Suncast resin bed should last 10-15 years without structural failure, assuming you don't live somewhere with brutal temperature swings. That's meaningful if you hate rebuilding, but it's not a 'lifetime investment' purchase. If longevity is your only concern, metal raised beds actually outlast resin by several years.

Is 12 inches deep enough for vegetable gardening?

For most vegetables—tomatoes, peppers, lettuce, spinach, squash—12 inches is adequate but barely. Root vegetables like carrots and beets prefer 14-18 inches. If you're growing shallow-rooted herbs and greens primarily, 12 inches works. If you want a true all-purpose vegetable bed, this depth will frustrate you by mid-season.

What's the actual cost-per-year when you factor in durability?

At typical price points ($150-250), spread over 12 years of realistic use, you're looking at $12-21 per year. That's cheaper than replacing a wood bed every 5 years ($30-50 per year), but more expensive than a basic wood option if you don't mind the maintenance cycle. The math works in Suncast's favor if you hate annual upkeep; it doesn't if budget is your only metric.
