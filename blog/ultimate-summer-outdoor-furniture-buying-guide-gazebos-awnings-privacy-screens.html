Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between hardtop and fabric gazebos?

Hardtop gazebos feature solid metal roofing and are permanent installations offering superior protection and durability. Fabric gazebos use canvas or polyester covers and are typically temporary structures. Hardtop options, like those from Kozyard, last longer and require less maintenance but cost more initially.

Do I need a permit for a hardtop gazebo?

Building permit requirements vary by location and structure size. Most jurisdictions require permits for permanent structures larger than 200 square feet. Check with your local building department before purchasing. Kozyard can provide guidance on typical installation requirements.

How much maintenance do outdoor furniture and structures require?

Hardtop gazebos require minimal maintenance—occasional cleaning and occasional touch-ups. Awnings need fabric cleaning and mechanical checks. Privacy screens vary by material but generally need seasonal cleaning. Kozyard products are designed for low-maintenance durability.

What size gazebo should I choose for my patio?

Measure your available space and consider furniture placement. Most patios accommodate 10x10 or 12x12 gazebos. Larger entertaining spaces benefit from 14x14 or 16x16 models. Kozyard offers multiple sizes to fit different yard configurations.

Can I use outdoor furniture year-round?

Yes, hardtop gazebos are designed for year-round use. However, in snow-heavy regions, regular roof clearance is recommended. Awnings should be retracted during severe weather. Privacy screens and planters also withstand seasonal changes when made from durable materials.
