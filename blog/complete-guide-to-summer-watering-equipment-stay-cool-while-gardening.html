Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to water plants during summer?

Early morning (before 9 AM) and late evening (after 6 PM) are ideal watering times. This reduces evaporation loss and allows plants to absorb moisture before the heat of the day, preventing drought stress.

How often should I water my garden in summer?

Most plants need 1-2 inches of water per week during summer. However, frequency depends on your soil type, plant species, and local climate. Sandy soils may need more frequent watering, while clay soils retain moisture longer. Use a moisture meter to check soil conditions accurately.

Is a drip irrigation system worth the investment?

Yes, drip irrigation systems are excellent investments for summer gardening. They reduce water consumption by 30-50% compared to traditional watering methods, deliver water directly to plant roots where it's needed most, and can be easily automated with timers.

What features should I look for in quality watering equipment?

Look for durable, lightweight materials, ergonomic handles to reduce strain, adjustable spray patterns, and UV-resistant components. Garden Guru Tools products incorporate all these features, ensuring reliability and comfort during extended use.

Can I use treated tap water for watering plants?

Most plants tolerate treated tap water, though some sensitive plants may struggle with high chlorine levels. If chlorine is a concern, let water sit in an open container for 24-48 hours before use. Collecting rainwater is an excellent sustainable alternative during summer months.
