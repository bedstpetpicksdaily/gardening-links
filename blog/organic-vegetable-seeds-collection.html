Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to plant organic vegetable seeds?

Planting times vary by vegetable type and your USDA hardiness zone. Cool-season crops like lettuce and broccoli thrive in spring and fall, while warm-season vegetables like tomatoes and peppers need soil temperatures above 60°F. Check seed packet instructions for specific timing.

Can I use organic seeds in containers and raised beds?

Absolutely! Organic seeds work excellently in containers and raised beds. Ensure you use quality potting soil or garden mix, provide adequate drainage, and follow spacing guidelines on each seed packet for optimal growth.

How do I store leftover organic vegetable seeds?

Store unused seeds in a cool, dry place—ideally in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Most seeds remain viable for 2-5 years when stored properly, allowing you to reuse them for multiple growing seasons.

What's the difference between heirloom and hybrid organic seeds?

Heirloom seeds produce plants identical to their parents and seeds can be saved for future planting. Hybrid seeds offer traits like disease resistance but won't produce identical offspring. Both are available in organic varieties.
