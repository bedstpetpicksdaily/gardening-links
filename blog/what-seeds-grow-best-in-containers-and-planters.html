The Short Answer

Container gardening works best with compact, shallow-rooted plants that don't need extensive space to flourish. Leafy greens like lettuce and spinach, herbs such as basil and cilantro, determinate tomato varieties, peppers, and root vegetables like radishes excel in pots and planters. These seeds germinate reliably in containers when given adequate drainage, proper soil, and consistent moisture. Success depends more on container size and soil quality than the specific seed variety.

The Full Explanation

Leafy Greens and Lettuces

Leafy greens are ideal for container growing because they mature quickly and require minimal root depth. Lettuce, spinach, arugula, and kale can grow in containers as shallow as 6-8 inches deep. These seeds germinate within 7-10 days and are ready to harvest in 30-45 days, making them perfect for beginners. Multiple succession plantings can be made throughout the season in the same container.

Herbs

Herbs are among the most popular container garden choices. Basil, parsley, cilantro, chives, oregano, and thyme all grow exceptionally well in pots. Most herbs need only 6-12 inches of soil depth and prefer well-draining containers. They produce abundant foliage throughout the growing season and can be harvested continuously, making them economical choices for seed investment.

Tomatoes

Determinate (bush) tomato varieties are far superior to indeterminate types for containers. Varieties like 'Patio', 'Tiny Tim', and 'Bush Early Girl' grow 2-3 feet tall and produce full-sized fruit in 12-inch pots or larger. Choose containers with at least 5 gallons of soil capacity. These seeds take 6-8 weeks to produce transplant-ready seedlings and will fruit prolifically in containers with adequate sunlight.

Peppers

Both sweet and hot peppers thrive in containers, requiring 8-10 inch diameter pots minimum. Pepper seeds are slower to germinate (10-14 days) and slower to mature (60-90 days) compared to other container vegetables, but they produce abundant fruit over extended periods. Smaller ornamental pepper varieties can grow in containers as small as 5 inches deep.

Root Vegetables

Radishes, carrots, beets, and turnips grow well in containers with 8-12 inches of soil depth. Radishes are the fastest, maturing in just 25-30 days. Carrots and beets require deeper containers but produce excellent results. These seeds need consistent moisture to prevent splitting and cracking as they develop underground.

Beans and Peas

Bush varieties of beans and peas are container-friendly, though pole varieties require trellising. These seeds germinate quickly (5-7 days) and are reliable performers. They require containers at least 8-10 inches deep and benefit from warm soil temperatures. Peas prefer cooler spring and fall conditions, while beans need warmth for optimal growth.
