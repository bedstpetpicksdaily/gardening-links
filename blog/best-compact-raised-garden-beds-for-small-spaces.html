What to Look For

  • Footprint and Height Balance: Look for beds that measure 2-4 feet in length and width, with heights between 8-12 inches. This size gives you enough volume for root crops and perennials while remaining easy to reach from all sides without stepping into the bed. Taller isn't always better in small spaces—you want something that fits your patio, balcony, or corner garden without overwhelming the area.
  • Material Durability and Weight: Choose between cedar (naturally rot-resistant and attractive), composite materials (lightweight and long-lasting), or galvanized metal (sleek and compact). Avoid untreated wood that will deteriorate quickly, and consider weight if you're placing the bed on a balcony or deck where weight limits matter. Cedar and composite are ideal because they won't leach chemicals into your soil and won't rust like steel can.
  • Drainage and Depth for Specific Plants: Ensure your compact bed has drainage holes or sits elevated enough for water to escape. For small spaces, choose a bed at least 8 inches deep for shallow-rooted herbs and greens, or 10-12 inches if you plan to grow carrots, beets, or peppers. Deep enough matters just as much as footprint—shallow beds limit what you can grow.
