Frequently Asked Questions

Does the B-hyve work with any WiFi setup, or does it require special 2.4GHz routers?

It requires 2.4GHz WiFi specifically—most modern routers broadcast both 2.4 and 5GHz bands simultaneously, so you're usually fine. However, if your router only broadcasts 5GHz, you'll need to enable the legacy 2.4GHz band or add a separate access point. I discovered this the hard way during my first setup, but once I switched my router settings, connection was rock-solid.

Can you run this on a well pump system, or is it strictly for municipal water/hose bib use?

The B-hyve is designed for standard hose bibs and municipal water pressure. If you're pulling from a well pump, the timer can work, but you'll need adequate pressure (typically 20-80 PSI). Low-pressure systems may cause the solenoid to stick or fail prematurely. Always check your well pump's output pressure before investing—a pressure gauge costs $15 and saves headaches.

How often do you actually need to replace batteries, and is weather-aware mode really worth enabling?

Battery life depends on how frequently the timer activates—with daily watering during growing season, I've gotten 18-24 months from standard AA batteries. Weather-aware mode absolutely justifies being enabled; it's saved me hundreds of gallons of water during unexpected rain. The only minor downside is that it requires accurate local weather data, so accuracy depends on how close the nearest weather station is to your garden.
