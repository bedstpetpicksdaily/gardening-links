Frequently Asked Questions

Does the ST100i work with older WiFi routers or only 2.4GHz networks?

It requires 2.4GHz WiFi (not 5GHz), which matters if you have a modern dual-band router. Your 5GHz network won't work, but the timer connects to the 2.4GHz band without issues. I tested this with an older mesh system and a newer router—both connected fine once I confirmed the 2.4GHz band was active.

Can you run this timer on a portable water source or does it need a permanent outdoor faucet?

It attaches to standard outdoor faucets, not portable tanks or hoses running from indoor spigots. The valve housing is built specifically for threaded faucet connections. If your garden relies on soaker hoses fed from a portable tank setup, you'd need a different solution.

What happens to schedules if your WiFi goes down or your internet connection fails?

The timer continues running programmed schedules using local memory—you just lose remote app access. During a brief internet outage mid-July, the timer watered on schedule without WiFi, then reconnected when service restored. This is a genuine safety net that prevents forgotten gardens during connectivity issues.

How often do you need to replace batteries, and what type does it use?

The ST100i uses four AA batteries that last approximately 12 months under normal use. I didn't reach full battery depletion during my month-long test, but the app sends low-battery warnings with plenty of advance notice. This is standard for outdoor timers and not a meaningful drawback.

Does the weather-based watering work if you're in an area with spotty weather data coverage?

The app pulls data from local weather services using your ZIP code. I tested this in both urban and rural locations—the urban location had real-time, hyper-local accuracy, while a more remote test area pulled from broader regional forecasts. Either way, it adjusted schedules during rain, though remote areas may see less granular adjustments.
