Frequently Asked Questions

Will the Gardena SmartWater sensor work if my phone is inside the house?

Mostly yes, depending on distance and barriers. I tested it from my kitchen roughly 30 feet from the sensor through two walls—notifications arrived reliably. Beyond 40-50 feet or through multiple concrete barriers, connection becomes spotty. If your garden is directly adjacent to your home, you're fine. If it's at the far end of a large property, expect occasional missed notifications.

How often should I actually check the soil, or does the app handle everything?

The app handles alerts beautifully, but manual spot-checks are still worth doing weekly. I found that sensor readings sometimes lagged during heavy rain or right after watering—the soil around the probe stays wet longer than the surrounding soil. Cross-referencing with a quick hand check took 30 seconds and prevented over-reliance on any single data point.

Is this better than just watering on a schedule?

Absolutely, especially in July. Scheduled watering ignores whether you got three inches of rain yesterday or endured a dry heat wave. The sensor adapts to actual conditions. I switched one bed from fixed scheduling to sensor-based watering and cut water usage by about 25% while improving plant health. The time savings come from trusting the alerts instead of manually inspecting every bed daily.
