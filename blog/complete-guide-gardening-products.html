Frequently Asked Questions

What gardening tools do I really need as a beginner?

Start with the essentials: a hand trowel, pruning shears, garden fork, spade, and a quality hose with spray nozzle. These five tools cover 90% of basic gardening tasks like planting, weeding, digging, and watering without overwhelming your shed or budget.

Which soil amendment is best for my vegetable garden?

Compost or aged manure are your best choices because they improve both soil structure and nutrient content while being cost-effective. If your soil drains poorly, add peat moss or coconut coir; if it's too acidic, add lime; if too alkaline, add sulfur.

What's the difference between organic and synthetic fertilizers?

Synthetic fertilizers provide nutrients immediately but don't improve soil structure, while organic fertilizers (compost, fish emulsion, bone meal) release nutrients slowly and build long-term soil health. Organic is better for sustainable gardening and edible plants, but synthetics work faster for quick nutrient boosts.

Do I need to buy a raised bed kit or can I build my own?

Building your own from untreated cedar or composite wood costs 30-50% less than pre-made kits and takes only an hour with basic tools. However, kits are convenient if you lack tools or prefer hassle-free assembly, though quality varies between brands.
