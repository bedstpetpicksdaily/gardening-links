Buying Guide: 3 Things to Look For

  • Material Durability: Choose between cedar, galvanized steel, or composite materials based on your climate and longevity needs. Steel and composite last longer in wet climates, while cedar offers natural beauty with good rot resistance.
  • Size and Depth: Most vegetables need at least 6-8 inches of soil depth, while root vegetables and tomatoes require 12 inches or more. Measure your space carefully and consider how much produce you want to grow.
  • Drainage and Base: Look for beds with proper drainage holes or a permeable base to prevent waterlogging. Some beds come with landscape fabric to prevent weeds while allowing water to drain.

FAQ About Raised Garden Beds

What's the best soil to use in a raised garden bed?

Use a high-quality garden soil or potting mix combined with compost for optimal results. A mixture of 50% topsoil, 30% compost, and 20% peat moss (or coconut coir) provides excellent drainage and nutrients for most vegetables and flowers.

How long do raised garden beds last?

Cedar beds typically last 10-15 years, galvanized steel beds can last 20+ years, and composite materials often outlast them all. The lifespan depends on your climate, maintenance, and the quality of materials used in construction.
