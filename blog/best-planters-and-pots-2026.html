Buying Guide: 3 Things to Look For

  • Drainage Holes: Proper drainage is essential to prevent root rot. Look for pots with drainage holes in the bottom, or ensure you can drill them yourself if needed.
  • Material Quality: Choose between ceramic, terracotta, plastic, concrete, or fabric based on your needs. Consider weather resistance, weight, and longevity for your growing conditions.
  • Size and Depth: Match pot size to your plant's root system. Larger plants need deeper pots, while shallow containers work better for succulents and herbs. Leave room for soil and water.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size planter do I need for my plant?

The general rule is to choose a pot that's 1-2 inches larger in diameter than your plant's current pot or root ball. For larger plants, you may need pots 12-18 inches or more. Always ensure adequate depth—most flowering plants need at least 8-12 inches of soil depth for healthy root development.

Do all planters need drainage holes?

Yes, drainage holes are crucial for plant health as they prevent water from pooling and causing root rot. If you love a decorative pot without drainage holes, use it as a cache pot and place a draining pot inside it, or drill holes carefully if the material allows.
