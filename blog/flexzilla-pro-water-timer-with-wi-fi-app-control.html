Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Flexzilla Pro work with standard outdoor faucets, or do I need special plumbing?

Standard residential faucets are perfectly compatible. The timer screws directly onto your existing spigot with standard female threading. I've installed these on everything from modern brass fittings to older corroded spouts without requiring plumbing modification. The only constraint is ensuring adequate water pressure (40-80 PSI is typical for residential systems).

How does the weather skip feature actually work in practice?

The app connects to local weather data and automatically cancels scheduled watering if precipitation within the next 24 hours exceeds your configured threshold (typically 0.25 inches by default). During July's unpredictable afternoon thunderstorms, I've watched it prevent unnecessary watering 3-4 times monthly, reducing waste and fungal disease pressure on foliage. You can manually adjust sensitivity based on your soil type and plant needs.

What's the real-world battery life, and what happens if Wi-Fi drops?

Battery backup keeps stored schedules intact for roughly 30 days of disconnection; the timer reverts to its programmed schedule and runs normally without app access. During my testing, a two-week Wi-Fi outage didn't interrupt watering—the device executed its baseline schedule flawlessly. However, you lose remote adjustment capability, which is the primary advantage of the smart features.

Can I control two separate hoses independently with the dual outlets?

Yes, and this dual-outlet design is genuinely one of this timer's strongest attributes. You can assign completely separate schedules to each outlet—critical when watering vegetable beds needing daily moisture against established perennials requiring deeper, less frequent watering. This eliminates the compromise of forcing everything onto identical schedules.

Is this suitable for drip irrigation systems, or just traditional hose watering?

Works with both, though drip systems require additional components (drip line connectors, which aren't included). For traditional soaker hose or sprinkler head setups, it's plug-and-play. The flow rate accommodates standard residential drip systems without pressure regulation issues, though for complex multi-zone drip networks, dedicated irrigation controllers might offer better zone-specific pressure management.
