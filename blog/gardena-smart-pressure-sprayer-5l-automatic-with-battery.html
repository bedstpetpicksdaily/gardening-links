Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the battery actually last on a full charge?

Real-world battery life ranges from 60–90 minutes of continuous spraying, depending on pressure setting and spray pattern selection. Lower pressure settings extend runtime noticeably. If you have a garden requiring more than 90 minutes of watering, having a second battery on hand is practical. Most users find one charge sufficient for their typical watering session, but check your garden size before assuming a single battery covers your entire routine.

Is this appropriate for indoor plant watering too?

Technically yes, but it's not optimized for that use. The 5-liter capacity and spray patterns are designed for outdoor volume. Indoor plant enthusiasts will likely find the pressure settings harder to dial down fine enough for delicate ferns or African violets without overshooting. This is primarily an outdoor/patio tool—there are better options if indoor watering is your main need.

What's the difference between this and cheaper manual pump sprayers?

Manual sprayers require constant hand engagement and pressure drops as the tank empties, forcing you to pump harder mid-session. The Gardena Smart removes that fatigue entirely and maintains consistent pressure throughout. For gardens under 10 containers, manual sprayers work fine. Beyond that, the Gardena's battery operation becomes genuinely valuable. You're also paying for pressure regulation and smart battery management—those features cost more but deliver measurable convenience improvements.
