Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to prune garden plants?

Most ornamental shrubs and trees benefit from pruning in late winter or early spring before new growth begins. Summer is ideal for deadheading flowers and removing damaged branches. Avoid heavy pruning in fall, as it encourages tender new growth vulnerable to frost damage. Always check specific plant varieties, as timing varies for different species.

How often should I sharpen my pruning shears?

For regular home gardeners, sharpening pruning shears 2-3 times per growing season is typically sufficient. If you notice the blades becoming dull or leaving ragged cuts, it's time to sharpen. Garden Guru Tools offers sharpening stones and maintenance kits specifically designed for their pruning equipment.

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruners?

Bypass pruners work like scissors with two curved blades that pass each other, making clean cuts on live, green stems. Anvil pruners feature one blade that cuts against a flat surface, making them better for cutting dead or hard wood. Garden Guru Tools produces both types, allowing you to choose based on your primary pruning needs.

Can quality pruning tools really improve plant health?

Yes. Clean cuts from sharp tools heal faster and reduce disease susceptibility compared to rough cuts from dull blades. Quality pruning tools also allow more precise trimming, which directs plant growth and improves overall appearance. Investing in tools from Garden Guru Tools directly impacts the long-term health and beauty of your garden.
