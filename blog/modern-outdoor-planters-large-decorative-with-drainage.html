Frequently Asked Questions

What size large outdoor planter do I need for trees and shrubs?

For most trees and shrubs, choose planters at least 18-24 inches deep and 18+ inches wide to provide adequate root space and stability. Larger plants like ornamental trees typically need 24-30+ inch diameter containers to prevent root binding and allow proper growth over several seasons.

Do large decorative outdoor planters need drainage holes?

Yes, drainage holes are essential to prevent root rot and waterlogging in outdoor planters. Even with drainage holes, use a quality potting mix (not garden soil) and ensure water can flow freely through the bottom of the container.

What material is best for large outdoor planters that last?

Fiberglass, composite resin, and high-quality ceramic are durable options that resist cracking in freezing temperatures and UV damage. Concrete and stone planters are heavy but extremely long-lasting, though they may require sealing in harsh climates.

How do I prevent large outdoor planters from becoming waterlogged in rain?

Ensure the planter has adequate drainage holes and add a 1-2 inch layer of gravel or broken pottery at the bottom before filling with potting soil. In areas with heavy rainfall, consider slightly raising planters on feet or a platform to improve water runoff.
