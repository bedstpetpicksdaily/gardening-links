The Short Answer

Spring is the perfect time to plant cool-season crops that thrive in temperatures between 40-70°F. Lettuce, spinach, peas, broccoli, cabbage, carrots, and radishes are excellent choices that germinate quickly and tolerate light frosts. Warm-season seeds like tomatoes, peppers, squash, and beans should be started indoors 6-8 weeks before your last frost date, or directly sown once soil temperatures reach 60°F or higher. The key is matching seed timing to your local climate and frost dates.

The Full Explanation

Spring gardening offers a unique window of opportunity for growing two distinct categories of vegetables. Understanding which seeds belong in each category will maximize your harvest and reduce frustration.

Cool-Season Spring Seeds

Cool-season crops are your spring superstars. These vegetables actually prefer the cooler temperatures of early spring and will bolt (flower prematurely) or develop bitter flavors once summer heat arrives. Plant these directly in your garden beds or raised garden beds as soon as soil is workable:

  • Leafy Greens: Lettuce, spinach, arugula, and kale germinate in soil as cool as 40°F and produce tender leaves within 30-60 days
  • Cruciferous Vegetables: Broccoli, cabbage, and cauliflower need 6-8 weeks but tolerate frost and develop better flavor in cool weather
  • Root Vegetables: Carrots, radishes, beets, and turnips germinate reliably in spring soil and improve in taste with cool-season growth
  • Legumes: Peas and broad beans can be direct-seeded as early as 4 weeks before the last frost date

Warm-Season Spring Seeds

Warm-season crops need soil temperatures of at least 60°F to germinate reliably, which typically occurs 1-2 weeks after your last spring frost date. Starting these seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before your last frost gives you a head start:

  • Tomatoes: Start indoors in late winter, transplant after frost danger passes
  • Peppers: Require consistent warmth; start indoors 8-10 weeks before last frost
  • Squash and Cucumbers: Wait until soil is consistently warm, or start indoors 4 weeks before planting out
  • Beans: Direct-seed only after all frost danger has passed and soil reaches 60°F

Spring Planting Timeline Strategy

The most successful spring gardeners use a staggered approach. Plant cool-season seeds immediately when soil is workable in early spring. Then, as spring progresses and frost danger decreases, transition to warm-season seeds. This extends your growing season and prevents wasted garden space. Use your local frost date (available from your USDA zone or local cooperative extension) as your planning anchor point.
