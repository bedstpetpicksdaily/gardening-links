Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Gardman compare to a traditional kneeling pad or garden stool?

The Gardman is a hybrid: it functions as both a kneeler and a low seat, whereas pads are one-dimensional and stools don't have tool storage. At $10, it's cheaper than most dedicated stools ($25-40 range) and offers integrated pouches that pads lack. The trade-off is that kneeling pads often outlast the Gardman's cushion, and stools provide better back support if you're sitting upright for extended periods. Use the Gardman if you alternate between kneeling and perching; invest in a stool if you primarily sit.

Will the cushion hold up through a full growing season?

Based on the 67 reviews, most cushions perform well for 2-3 months of regular use, then begin noticeably compressing. If you garden 3-4 days per week, expect degradation by late August or September. For casual use (1-2 times weekly), it should remain adequately cushioned through the season. Keep it stored indoors during heavy rain—UV exposure and moisture accelerate breakdown of the foam.

Is the $10 price too good to be true, or is this a genuine deal?

It's genuinely affordable, not a sign of hidden defects. Gardman manufactures budget-friendly garden products with acceptable quality standards. At $10, the profit margin is minimal, meaning there's no incentive to use substandard materials. The low price reflects manufacturing efficiency and lower brand overhead, not corner-cutting. What you're not getting is premium durability or commercial-grade construction—that costs $40+. If you're comparing this to boutique ergonomic benches priced at $60-80, you're comparing different products entirely.
