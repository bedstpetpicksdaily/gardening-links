Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need to wait another month after Lomi finishes processing?

Yes. Lomi heats and dries your scraps, breaking them down mechanically, but it doesn't complete the biological decomposition that true compost requires. You can speed up the final stage by mixing the output directly into garden soil or a worm bin, where it'll finish breaking down in 4-6 weeks. Some gardeners bury it directly under fall plants and let nature handle it.

How much does it cost to run compared to a regular outdoor bin?

An electric composter uses roughly 0.3-0.5 kWh per cycle. At average U.S. electricity rates, that's about $0.04-$0.07 per cycle. If you run it daily, you're looking at $1.20-$2.10 monthly. A regular compost bin costs zero to operate, but an outdoor bin doesn't fit in every living situation, and it requires active management. The trade-off is convenience versus cost.

Can it handle meat, dairy, and oils?

No—and this is critical. Stick to fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, and paper products. Meat, dairy, oils, and pet waste will create odor problems and potentially break down improperly. If you generate significant animal protein waste, this tool isn't your solution.

Is the 4.3-star rating accurate, or are people just being nice?

The rating is realistic. Most 5-star reviews come from people with outdoor garden space who use the output regularly. Most complaints center on expecting finished compost immediately or frustration with the ongoing processing time. The people leaving 3 stars typically underestimated the learning curve or had unrealistic expectations about output speed.
