What Are the Best Seeds to Plant in Spring?

The best seeds to plant in spring include cool-season crops like lettuce, peas, and spinach in early spring, followed by warm-season favorites like tomatoes, peppers, beans, and squash after the last frost date. Your specific choices depend on your USDA hardiness zone, local frost dates, and whether you prefer vegetables, herbs, or flowers.

The Short Answer

Spring planting success comes down to timing and your local climate. Cool-season crops tolerate frost and should go in the ground as soon as soil is workable, typically 2-3 weeks before your last spring frost. Warm-season crops need soil temperatures above 60°F and should wait until after your final frost date. The most reliable spring seeds include peas, lettuce, broccoli, tomatoes, peppers, beans, squash, and basil.

The Full Explanation

Cool-Season Crops (Plant Early Spring)

Cool-season vegetables germinate in cold soil and actually prefer temperatures between 40-60°F. These should be your first plantings:

  • Peas - One of the earliest crops; plant 4-6 weeks before last frost
  • Lettuce and Leafy Greens - Spinach, kale, arugula, and chard sprout quickly in cool weather
  • Broccoli and Cabbage - Start from transplants for faster results
  • Carrots and Beets - Direct sow seeds 2-3 weeks before last frost
  • Radishes - Fast-growing and perfect for impatient gardeners (ready in 3-4 weeks)
  • Onions and Garlic - Plant sets or cloves early for summer harvest

Warm-Season Crops (Plant After Last Frost)

These heat-loving plants need soil consistently warmer than 60°F and air temperatures above 50°F at night. Wait until your frost danger has completely passed:

  • Tomatoes - The most popular garden vegetable; start from transplants for best results
  • Peppers - Require warm soil; plant transplants after soil reaches 65°F
  • Beans - Direct sow seeds after soil warms; they germinate best at 70°F+
  • Squash and Zucchini - Plant seeds directly in warm soil 1-2 inches deep
  • Cucumbers - Thrive in heat; wait for consistently warm nights
  • Corn - Plant seeds directly when soil reaches 60°F
  • Basil and Other Warm Herbs - Start transplants after frost danger passes
