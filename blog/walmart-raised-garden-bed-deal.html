What to Look For

Material Quality and Durability

  • Cedar or composite materials resist rot better than pine (lasts 10+ years vs. 3-5 years)
  • Check for untreated wood to avoid harmful chemical leaching into vegetables
  • Galvanized metal frames offer rust resistance and longevity
  • Avoid treated lumber with CCA (chromated copper arsenate) preservatives

Size and Depth Specifications

  • Minimum 6-inch depth for herbs and lettuce; 8-12 inches for vegetables
  • Standard 4x8 ft dimensions maximize growing space while staying manageable
  • Measure your yard space before purchasing—common regret is oversizing
  • Consider accessibility width (4 ft allows reaching center from both sides)

Assembly and Installation Requirements

  • Pre-assembled options cost 20-30% more but save 2-3 hours of setup
  • Check if corner brackets are included—they're often sold separately
  • Verify soil capacity requirements (a 4x8x12" bed needs ~32 cubic feet of soil)
  • Look for integrated drainage systems to prevent waterlogging

Warranty and Return Policy

  • Walmart's current 90-day return window covers seasonal buyers well
  • Manufacturer warranties on wood typically range from 1-5 years
  • Check whether structural damage from weather is covered
  • Document condition photos immediately upon delivery
