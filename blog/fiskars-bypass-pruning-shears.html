Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruning shears?

Bypass shears have two curved blades that pass each other like scissors, making clean cuts ideal for live plants. Anvil shears have one blade that cuts against a flat surface, better suited for dead wood. Bypass shears are preferred for most gardening tasks.

What size branches can Fiskars bypass shears cut?

Fiskars bypass pruning shears typically cut branches up to ¾ inch in diameter. For thicker branches, you may need lopping shears or a pruning saw.

How do I maintain Fiskars bypass pruning shears?

Clean blades after each use, wipe away sap with rubbing alcohol, and sharpen regularly with a sharpening tool. Occasionally apply oil to the pivot point to maintain smooth operation.

Are Fiskars pruning shears suitable for all plant types?

Yes, they work well on most flowering plants, shrubs, and small trees. They're ideal for roses, perennials, ornamental plants, and general garden maintenance in raised beds and landscapes.
