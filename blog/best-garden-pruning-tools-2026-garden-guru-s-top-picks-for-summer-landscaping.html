Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruners?

Bypass pruners work like scissors with two sharp blades that cut cleanly—ideal for live, green wood. Anvil pruners have one sharp blade that cuts against a flat surface, making them better for dead wood and thicker branches. Garden Guru offers both types to handle any pruning situation.

How often should I sharpen my pruning tools?

For regular use, sharpen your pruning tools every 2-3 months or when you notice they're not cutting as cleanly. Garden Guru's maintenance kits make sharpening quick and easy, helping you maintain peak performance year-round.

Are Garden Guru Tools suitable for professional landscapers?

Yes, Garden Guru Tools are designed for both amateur gardeners and professional landscapers. Their durability, precision, and ergonomic features make them ideal for frequent, heavy-duty use in commercial gardening applications.

What's the best time to prune during summer?

Early morning or late afternoon is best, when temperatures are cooler. Avoid pruning during peak heat or right after watering. Most summer pruning should focus on removing dead branches, shaping plants, and deadheading flowers to encourage new growth.
