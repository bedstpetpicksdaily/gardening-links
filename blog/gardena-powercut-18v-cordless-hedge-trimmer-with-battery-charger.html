Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Gardena PowerCut handle thicker branches compared to gas hedge trimmers?

The 18V system cuts branches up to ¾-inch reliably, which matches most consumer gas models. Where gas pulls ahead is on branches thicker than an inch or on extremely dense evergreens—those situations belong to different equipment. For standard hedge maintenance (privet, boxwood, yew), the cordless approach is genuinely sufficient. The advantage is you eliminate starting hassles, maintenance intervals, and fuel storage.

Is the included battery adequate or should I buy a second one?

For a single hedge row or moderate backyard work, the included 2.5Ah battery handles 40-45 minutes of cutting comfortably. If your property has multiple hedge sections or you're the type who tackles yard work in one marathon session, a second battery is a smart investment. One battery isn't limiting—it's just realistic about typical homeowner usage patterns. The charger is reasonably fast at roughly 1 hour for a full charge, so swapping batteries mid-project is practical.

How does noise level compare to other cordless trimmers?

At approximately 85dB, the Gardena sits slightly below most electric hedge trimmers and dramatically below gas equipment. That means early-morning or evening trimming won't upset neighbors. It's not silent like hand shears, but the reduction in noise compared to gas is genuinely noticeable and compounds if you're doing weekend yard work regularly. This feature alone converts skeptics once they use it.
