What to Look For

  • Untreated or naturally rot-resistant wood: Beginners should avoid pressure-treated lumber that contains harmful chemicals. Instead, opt for cedar, redwood, or composite materials that naturally resist rot without toxic preservatives. This keeps your soil and vegetables safe while requiring less maintenance headaches down the road.
  • Simple assembly and modest dimensions: Look for beds that are 4x8 feet or smaller, with straightforward corner joints and pre-drilled holes if possible. Oversized beds become difficult to reach the center, and complicated assembly can discourage you before you even plant your first seed.
  • Adequate depth for root vegetables: A minimum of 12 inches is fine for shallow-rooted plants like lettuce and herbs, but 18-24 inches gives you flexibility to grow carrots, potatoes, and peppers without restrictions. This versatility helps you succeed with whatever you want to plant.
