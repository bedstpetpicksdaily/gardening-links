Understanding PSI and GPM: The Core Specifications

The two most important metrics for any electric pressure washer are PSI (pounds per square inch) and GPM (gallons per minute). PSI measures the force of water pressure, while GPM indicates water flow rate. For most residential cleaning tasks—including patios, decks, and light driveway work—a pressure washer with 1,500-2,000 PSI and 1.5-2.0 GPM is ideal. For tougher jobs like removing stubborn stains or cleaning concrete, consider models with 2,500-3,000 PSI.

Motor Type and Build Quality Matter

Electric pressure washers come with either induction or universal motors. Induction motors are quieter, more durable, and require less maintenance, making them ideal for residential use. Universal motors are lighter and more compact but tend to be noisier and have shorter lifespans. When evaluating brands, look for robust construction, sealed motors, and quality hose connections that won't leak or degrade over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What PSI do I need for cleaning my driveway?

For driveways, aim for 2,500-3,000 PSI combined with 2.5+ GPM. This power level effectively removes dirt, oil stains, and mildew without damaging concrete surfaces when used properly.

Can I use an electric pressure washer on wood decks?

Yes, but use lower PSI settings (1,200-1,500 PSI) and maintain proper nozzle distance to avoid splintering wood. Most electric models like those from Giraffe Tools allow pressure adjustment for delicate surfaces.

How long does an electric pressure washer cord need to be?

Standard cords are 25-35 feet, but you can use a heavy-duty outdoor extension cord for additional reach. Always use a GFCI-protected outlet for safety when operating electric power tools near water.

Do electric pressure washers require maintenance?

Minimal maintenance is required—mostly just rinsing internal components after use and storing properly. No oil changes or spark plug replacements needed unlike gas models, making them ideal for casual homeowners.
