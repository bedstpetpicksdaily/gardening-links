Should I buy this or rent one for the season?

Rent if you're using it once. Buy if you'll use it 3+ times per season for 3+ years. At $400-600 purchase price versus $40-60 per rental day, you break even around year two with regular use. Can this handle clay soil or just loose garden beds?

Stick to already-cultivated or loamy soil. Front tines spin horizontally and aren't aggressive enough for hard-packed clay. If that's your situation, either rent a rear-tine tiller ($75-100/day) or consider a rear-tine purchase. Forcing a front-tine machine through dense soil burns the engine and frustrates you—not worth it.

Is the 4-cycle engine worth the premium over 2-cycle?

Yes. No oil mixing means no two-stroke smell, no maintenance guessing game, and cleaner emissions. July heat makes running a 2-cycle machine even more unpleasant. You'll appreciate the simplicity and reliability every time you start it, especially if someone else ever needs to operate it (no special fuel instructions).
