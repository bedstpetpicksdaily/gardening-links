Do I need to test my soil before planting vegetables in Garden Grove?

Yes, soil testing is recommended, especially if you live within 1-2 miles of the spill location or have an older garden. Contact your local county extension office for affordable soil testing kits that screen for chemical contaminants and heavy metals.

What vegetables are safe to grow after a chemical spill?

Root vegetables and leafy greens absorb contaminants more readily than fruiting plants like tomatoes or squash. If soil testing shows acceptable contamination levels, fruiting crops are safer choices; raised beds with clean soil are the safest option regardless of location.

Should I use raised beds or in-ground gardens in Garden Grove right now?

Raised beds with imported, uncontaminated soil are the safest approach until soil testing confirms your ground is clean. This eliminates contamination risks entirely and gives you immediate growing options without waiting for clearance results.
