Is stainless steel really necessary, or is high-carbon steel fine?

Stainless avoids rust entirely, which matters if you live near coastal areas, have humid summers, or store tools outdoors. High-carbon steel is stronger and lighter but requires oiling and storage discipline—you're basically preventing rust rather than being immune to it. For July-through-October use patterns, stainless saves headaches. For winter storage in dry sheds, high-carbon works fine. Gardman's stainless choice simplifies ownership, though it comes at a slight premium.

Will these handles really crack after two years like some reviews claim?

Hardwood cracking depends entirely on storage and climate. If tools dry out in a garage during winter with no moisture, wood shrinks and splits. If they're regularly cleaned and stored in humid conditions or indoors, cracking is rare. The 4.3-star rating suggests most people aren't experiencing cracks—that's the 10-15% who stored tools carelessly. Treat hardwood handles like any wood tool: keep them conditioned lightly with linseed oil once yearly, and they'll stay tight for 4-6 years minimum.
