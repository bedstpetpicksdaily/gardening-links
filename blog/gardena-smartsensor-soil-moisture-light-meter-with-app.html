Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Gardena SmartSensor work offline or require constant WiFi?

It requires WiFi to sync data to the app, but the sensor itself stores readings locally. If your connection drops, you can still physically check the device display—it has a small LCD screen. However, the whole point of buying this is app monitoring, so poor WiFi defeats the value proposition.

How accurate are the moisture readings compared to soil testing?

Accurate enough for practical garden use, but not laboratory-grade. It measures relative moisture (0-100%) rather than absolute water content. For most plants, this is sufficient. The bigger issue: sensor accuracy drifts slightly over months of soil contact, so don't treat readings as gospel—use them as trends instead.

Can you use multiple sensors with one account?

Yes, and this is where the tool actually justifies its cost. You can deploy sensors across different garden zones and monitor all of them from one app. If you're managing a vegetable garden, ornamental beds, and container plants simultaneously, tracking separate moisture needs becomes genuinely valuable in July heat.

Will this sensor work in potted plants or just raised beds?

Works in both. The probe is short enough for containers (though deeper pots give more reliable readings). Keep in mind that potted plants dry faster, so more frequent monitoring matters—where the app convenience actually adds value over checking every day manually.
