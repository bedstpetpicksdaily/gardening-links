Frequently Asked Questions

How deep does the probe need to go into the soil?

The recommended depth is 4-6 inches, which captures the zone where most plant roots actively absorb water. Shallow placement (1-2 inches) gives you early warning of surface drying but misses what's happening at root depth. Deep placement (8+ inches) measures the subsoil, useful for larger trees but less practical for vegetable gardens. Mark your insertion depth with tape so you can maintain consistency if you ever need to relocate it.

Does the Aquastat work with non-Gardena watering systems?

Yes, but with limitations. It outputs a wireless signal that most smart home hubs (SmartThings, Home Assistant, etc.) can receive, but you'll lose some of Gardena's automated watering logic. Integration with third-party valves requires custom automation rules. It works best as a data source for existing smart home systems rather than a standalone controller for non-Gardena systems. If you're mixing brands, expect to do more manual setup.

How often do I need to replace batteries or recalibrate?

Battery life typically lasts 12-18 months depending on WiFi signal strength and check frequency. The sensor doesn't require manual calibration—Gardena calibrated it at the factory. However, soil composition changes over seasons. If you amend heavily or add mulch, the sensor may need a week or two to stabilize its readings in the new soil profile. This isn't a recalibration issue; it's normal physics. Plan on replacing batteries in late spring before the hot months hit.
