Maintenance Tips to Extend Tool Life

Proper care extends your pruning tools' lifespan significantly. Clean blades after each use with a dry cloth to prevent sap buildup and rust. Sharpen blades regularly using a whetstone or professional sharpening service—dull tools require more force and cause plant damage. Store tools in a dry location, and oil wooden handles occasionally to prevent cracking. Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to prune during summer?

Early morning or late afternoon pruning minimizes stress on plants. Avoid pruning during extreme heat. Focus on deadheading spent flowers, removing diseased branches, and shaping growth. Most plants benefit from light pruning throughout summer rather than heavy cutting.

How often should I sharpen my pruning tools?

Sharpen pruning tool blades every 4-6 weeks during active gardening season, or whenever you notice crushed stems instead of clean cuts. Regular sharpening maintains efficiency and prevents plant damage from dull blades.

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruners?

Bypass pruners feature two curved blades that pass each other like scissors, ideal for clean cuts on living stems. Anvil pruners have one blade pressing against a flat surface, better suited for dead wood. Garden Guru Tools offers both types for different pruning tasks.

Can I use the same pruning tools for all plant types?

While versatile hand pruners work for most plants, specialized tools perform better for specific tasks. Hedge shears work best for shrubs, pruning saws for thick branches, and lopping shears for material between hand pruner and saw capacity. A complete toolkit ensures optimal results.
