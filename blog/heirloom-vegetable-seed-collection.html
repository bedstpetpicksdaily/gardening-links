Frequently Asked Questions

What makes heirloom seeds different from hybrid seeds?

Heirloom seeds are open-pollinated varieties that produce true-to-type offspring, allowing you to save seeds for future seasons. Hybrid seeds create genetically different plants and must be repurchased annually. Heirlooms offer superior flavor and genetic diversity that benefits long-term garden sustainability.

Can I grow heirloom vegetables in raised garden beds?

Yes, heirloom vegetables thrive in raised garden beds. In fact, raised beds provide excellent drainage and soil control, which many heirloom varieties prefer. Ensure quality soil, proper spacing, and adequate irrigation for best results with your heirloom seeds.

Are all heirloom seeds organic?

Not all heirloom seeds are certified organic, though many are. Check product descriptions and certifications when purchasing. Our recommended selection includes both certified organic and conventional heirloom varieties to suit different gardening preferences.

How do I store heirloom vegetable seeds?

Store seeds in a cool, dry place ideally between 40-50°F with low humidity. An airtight container in a refrigerator or cool closet extends germination rates for several years. Proper storage ensures your investment in quality heirloom seeds maintains maximum viability.
