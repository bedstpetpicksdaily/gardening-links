Frequently Asked Questions

How should I store heirloom vegetable seeds to maintain germination rates?

Store seeds in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight. An airtight container in the refrigerator or freezer works best, maintaining temperatures between 35-50°F. Proper storage can keep seeds viable for 3-5+ years, allowing you to propagate your garden sustainably.

Can I use these seeds in raised garden beds and containers?

Yes, absolutely. Heirloom seeds grow well in raised beds, large planters, and containers. Choose varieties based on root depth requirements and ensure proper irrigation systems are in place for consistent watering and optimal growth.

Are heirloom seeds more difficult to grow than hybrid varieties?

No, heirloom seeds are generally just as easy to grow as hybrids. They often have excellent germination rates and many varieties are quite hardy. The main advantage is flavor and the ability to save seeds for next season's garden.

What climate zones are these heirloom vegetables suitable for?

This 30-pack includes varieties for different USDA hardiness zones and growing seasons. Check the included growing guide for each variety's specific zone requirements and planting times to ensure success in your garden.
