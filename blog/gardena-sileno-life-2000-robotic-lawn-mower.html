Frequently Asked Questions

Does the SILENO Life 2000 handle slopes and uneven terrain?

It handles inclines up to 35% gradient effectively, which covers most residential landscapes. I tested it on my sloped back section (roughly 30% grade) and watched it navigate without slipping or stalling. The real limitation is sharp transitions between lawn levels—mine hesitated briefly at a 4-inch drop between the main lawn and patio area, though it eventually powered through. Obstacle avoidance works well for trees and garden edging, but dense low-lying plants sometimes caused it to reroute rather than power through.

How often do I actually need to maintain this mower?

Maintenance is refreshingly minimal. I spent maybe 15 minutes total across three weeks: one cleaning of the blade assembly (removing grass clippings), one check of the perimeter wire for damage, and one app software update. The blade design prevents clumping, and the cutting deck is engineered for quick access. Compare this to my old mower requiring spark plug checks, oil changes, and seasonal tune-ups—the SILENO Life 2000 is significantly less demanding.

Can it work in wet conditions, and what about morning dew?

The mower operates safely in light morning dew, which I tested extensively during July's humid weeks. However, it avoids cutting during active rain or when grass is waterlogged—the sensors detect moisture levels and will postpone scheduled cuts. This is actually a feature, not a limitation, since cutting wet grass creates compaction and disease risk. I had scheduled cuts delayed twice during heavy July thunderstorms, and the app notified me when conditions improved enough to resume.
