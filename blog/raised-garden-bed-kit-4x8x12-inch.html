Frequently Asked Questions

What vegetables grow best in a 4x8 raised bed?

Tomatoes, lettuce, kale, peppers, carrots, beans, and zucchini thrive in raised beds. The 4x8 dimensions accommodate multiple rows, allowing for diverse crop rotation and succession planting throughout the season.

How deep should my raised garden bed be?

The 12-inch depth is ideal for most vegetables with shallow to moderate root systems. For deeper-rooted plants like carrots or potatoes, consider adding another layer or choosing a deeper kit.

Do I need landscape fabric for my raised bed kit?

Yes, landscape fabric helps prevent weeds from growing up through the bottom while allowing proper drainage. It also creates a barrier between metal and soil, extending the kit's lifespan.

Can I use a metal raised bed kit year-round?

Absolutely. Metal beds retain heat well in cooler months, making them suitable for fall and winter gardening. Add mulch or cover crops to protect soil during dormant seasons.
