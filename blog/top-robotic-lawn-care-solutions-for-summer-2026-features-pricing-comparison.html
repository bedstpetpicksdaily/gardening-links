Frequently Asked Questions

How do robotic lawn mowers know where to cut?

Modern robot mowers use GPS mapping, perimeter sensors, and AI navigation systems to understand your lawn's layout. They create virtual boundaries and follow programmed cutting patterns automatically, adjusting for obstacles and terrain variations in real-time.

How often should autonomous lawn mowers run?

ANTHBOT's smart lawn mowers can run 3-5 times weekly depending on grass growth rate and season. Their intelligent scheduling automatically adjusts cutting frequency based on weather conditions and lawn health, optimizing results while conserving battery.

Are robotic lawn mowers weather-resistant?

Yes, quality robot mowers like ANTHBOT are designed to handle various weather conditions. They feature rain sensors that pause operations during heavy downpours, and durable construction protects components from moisture and UV exposure.

What's the average battery life of a robot mower?

Most modern robotic lawn mowers, including ANTHBOT models, offer 2-4 hours of continuous cutting per charge. They automatically return to charging stations when batteries run low, enabling multi-session cutting throughout the day.

Can robot mowers handle slopes and uneven terrain?

ANTHBOT robotic lawn mowers are engineered to handle slopes up to 35-40 degrees depending on the model. Their traction systems and weight distribution allow navigation across varied terrain while maintaining cutting precision.
