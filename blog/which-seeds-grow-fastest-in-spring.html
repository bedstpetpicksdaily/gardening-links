Which Seeds Grow Fastest in Spring

Radishes, lettuce, spinach, and peas are among the fastest-growing seeds in spring, typically sprouting within 3-7 days and reaching maturity in 20-40 days. These cool-season crops thrive in spring's mild temperatures and shorter daylight hours, making them ideal for gardeners seeking quick results.

The Short Answer

Fast-growing spring seeds fall into two categories: leafy greens and root vegetables. Radishes are the speediest, germinating in just 3-5 days and maturing in as little as 20-25 days. Lettuce, spinach, arugula, and peas follow closely behind, all ready for harvest within 30-45 days of planting. These crops prefer cooler temperatures between 50-70°F, which makes spring the perfect season for rapid growth.

The Full Explanation

Why Spring is Ideal for Fast-Growing Seeds

Spring provides the optimal conditions for rapid seed germination and growth. Soil temperatures are rising but still cool enough to prevent bolting in leafy greens, while increasing daylight hours fuel photosynthesis. Consistent moisture from spring rains and relatively mild temperatures create a Goldilocks environment for seeds that would struggle in summer heat.

Top Fast-Growing Spring Seeds

Radishes are the undisputed champions of speed. They germinate in 3-5 days and reach full maturity in 20-30 days. Cherry Belle and French Breakfast varieties are particularly quick. The key to success is direct sowing into well-prepared garden beds or raised garden beds with loose, well-draining soil.

Lettuce germinates in 7-10 days and matures in 30-45 days depending on the variety. Loose-leaf varieties like Buttercrunch and Oakleaf grow faster than head lettuces. Lettuce prefers temperatures around 60-65°F and benefits from consistent moisture and partial shade as temperatures rise.

Spinach sprouts in 7-14 days and reaches harvest size in 40-50 days. It tolerates colder soil than most vegetables and can be direct sown as soon as soil is workable in spring. Space plants 3-6 inches apart in rows for maximum production.

Peas (both snap and snow varieties) germinate in 10-14 days and flower within 40-60 days. They fix nitrogen in soil, improving conditions for subsequent plantings. Soak seeds overnight before planting to accelerate germination.

Arugula germinates in 5-10 days and reaches harvest size in just 30-40 days. This spicy green is prolific and can be harvested leaf-by-leaf for continuous production throughout spring.

Other Fast Growers include turnips (30-50 days), beets (50-70 days), and chard (45-55 days). Microgreens deserve special mention—they can be ready for harvest in just 1-3 weeks, making them the fastest option if you're growing indoors in shallow planters.
