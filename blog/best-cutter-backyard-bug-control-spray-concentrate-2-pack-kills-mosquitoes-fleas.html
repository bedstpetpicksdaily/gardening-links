What to Look For When Buying Bug Control Spray Concentrate

1. Active Ingredient Strength

Look for the concentration of active ingredients (typically bifenthrin in professional-grade products). Higher concentrations mean better coverage and longer-lasting protection. Check if the product requires dilution and what ratio is recommended—this affects both cost-effectiveness and safety.

2. Coverage Area Per Mix Ratio

The best concentrates specify exactly how many square feet one ounce of concentrate will treat. Cutter products typically provide clear mixing instructions. A concentrate that covers 3,000+ square feet per mixed gallon offers excellent value. Compare coverage rates between brands to ensure you're getting maximum yard protection.

3. Safety Certifications and Pet/Child Safety

Verify the product is EPA-registered and check if it's safe for use around pets and children. Look for products that allow re-entry after a specified drying period (typically 30 minutes to a few hours). Read label warnings carefully regarding application to food gardens or vegetable areas.

4. Application Method Compatibility

Confirm the concentrate works with your equipment—whether that's a pump sprayer, hose-end sprayer, or tank sprayer. Not all concentrates are compatible with all application methods. Check customer reviews for specific equipment compatibility and ease of mixing instructions.
