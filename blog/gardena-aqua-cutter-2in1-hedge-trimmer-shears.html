Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Gardena Aqua Cutter compare to cheaper corded options?

Corded trimmers typically cost $30–50 less and deliver consistent power without battery worries. Trade-off: you're tied to a cord and can't switch blade modes. For small urban gardens or apartment patios, cordless loses its advantage. For sprawling properties where you're changing tasks frequently, the Gardena's flexibility justifies the extra cost.

Is the battery powerful enough for thick branches?

The reviews split here. Branches under ½ inch: no problem. ¾ inch and up: the motor slows noticeably, and you'll need sharp blades. Most users charge between jobs when tackling thick material. If your shrubs are mature and woody, budget extra charging time or consider a corded model with unlimited power.

What's the real maintenance cost over time?

Blade sharpening runs $10–15 per session, and you'll likely need it annually if you're using this regularly. Battery replacement after 3–4 years costs $40–60. Compare that to a $50 corded trimmer with zero battery costs—the total cost of ownership matters for budget planning. Factor in your usage frequency before committing.
