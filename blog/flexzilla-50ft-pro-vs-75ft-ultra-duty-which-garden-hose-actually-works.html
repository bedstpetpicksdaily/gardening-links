Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Flexzilla Pro really resist kinking better than standard rubber hoses?

Yes. Real-world testing shows both models bounce back at 90-degree angles around garden corners and remain supple after coiling in July heat (95°F+). Standard rubber hoses develop permanent creases; Flexzilla's reinforced design does not.

Is 50 feet enough, or should I go to 75 feet?

50 feet covers most residential yards and small garden zones. 75 feet is necessary for quarter-acre properties, multiple outdoor zones, or drip irrigation setups requiring extended reach without pressure loss. Measure your longest run from faucet to furthest watering point.

How does the 75ft hose hold up during hot summer months?

The Ultra Duty maintains flexibility at 95°F+ without degrading, splitting, or hardening. Rubber exterior doesn't degrade after daily exposure to direct sunlight over multiple growing seasons. Cold tolerance is equally strong near-freezing temperatures.

Are the brass fittings on the 75ft worth the extra cost?

Yes, for frequent users. Brass fittings are rated for 40+ connect-disconnect cycles without leaks and don't cross-thread like plastic connections. If you swap hoses between sprinklers or irrigation zones regularly, brass prevents leaks and frustration.

What's the actual weight, and is it manageable for regular use?

The 50ft Pro is lightweight and one-handed friendly. The 75ft Ultra Duty weighs 12–13 lbs—noticeably heavier but manageable for most gardeners with two-handed use. It remains coilable to practical storage size despite the 50% length increase.
