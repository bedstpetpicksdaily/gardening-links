Frequently Asked Questions

What size drip irrigation kit do I need?

Measure your garden beds and calculate total linear footage. Most kits come in 25-100 foot lengths, and many are expandable. For raised beds, plan for drip lines along each row with 12-18 inch spacing between emitters.

Can I use a drip irrigation timer with any kit?

Most timers are compatible with standard garden hose connections. Check that your kit includes a connector that fits your outdoor faucet, and ensure the timer has flow rate capabilities matching your system.

How often should I run my drip irrigation system?

Most gardens need 1-2 inches of water weekly. Run your system early morning for 30-60 minutes, adjusting based on rainfall, soil type, and plant requirements.

Do drip systems work for all outdoor plants?

Drip irrigation works best for vegetables, shrubs, trees, and perennials. Avoid using directly on plants preferring dry conditions. You can always adjust individual emitter flow rates.
