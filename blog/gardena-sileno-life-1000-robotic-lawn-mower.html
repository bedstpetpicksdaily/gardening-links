Frequently Asked Questions

How often does it actually need maintenance?

Real owners report minimal upkeep during the season: occasional blade sharpening every few months, clearing the undercarriage of debris after wet conditions, and keeping the sensors clean. The batteries hold up well for a full season in normal use. Winter storage requires draining fuel (if applicable) or letting the battery cycle down. It's not a set-and-forget machine for nine months, but it's significantly less demanding than traditional mowers.

Will it work on a quarter-acre lawn or is it overkill?

A quarter-acre is borderline. A traditional mower handles that in 20-30 minutes, so the time savings don't feel dramatic. The Sileno Life 1000 makes sense when you have between one-third and three-quarter acre, or when your lawn shape is complicated with obstacles. On tiny properties, you're paying premium price for modest convenience gains. Consider a compact corded electric mower instead.

What happens if it rains while the mower is out?

The Sileno Life 1000 handles light rain without issue, but it returns to base automatically in heavy downpours. Most owners find this actually works in their favor because wet grass clumps and clogs up traditional mower decks anyway. You're not sacrificing mowing quality—you're just distributing the work across more frequent, shorter cycles instead of one weekly blitz.

Can you hide the charging dock or does it look awful in your yard?

The docking station is visible and takes up space roughly the size of a car parking spot. Some owners integrate it into a shed opening or screen it with landscaping, but there's no way to truly hide it without defeating the automation. If aesthetics matter and you have a small visible yard, this is a real consideration. Larger properties usually have room to position it less prominently.

How does it compare to other brands at this price point?

Gardena's reputation for durability and app reliability is solid, and the 4.3-star rating suggests real-world satisfaction. Comparable alternatives from Husqvarna and WORX exist at similar prices, each with different wheel traction profiles and app interfaces. Your choice should depend on whether you prioritize terrain handling, app features, or local warranty support. Check what's available through your preferred retailers before deciding.
