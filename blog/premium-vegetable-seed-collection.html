Frequently Asked Questions

What makes heirloom seeds different from regular vegetable seeds?

Heirloom seeds are open-pollinated varieties passed down through generations, offering superior flavor and the ability to save seeds for replanting. Unlike hybrid seeds, they produce plants that breed true to type.

Can I use heirloom seeds in raised garden beds and containers?

Absolutely! Heirloom vegetable seeds work excellently in raised beds and containers. Just ensure proper soil depth, drainage, and spacing according to seed packet instructions for optimal growth.

How long do organic heirloom seeds stay viable?

When stored in cool, dry conditions (40-50°F with low humidity), most heirloom seeds remain viable for 3-5 years, though germination rates may gradually decline over time.

Are premium heirloom seeds worth the extra cost?

Yes, premium seeds offer higher germination rates, better disease resistance, and superior flavor. The investment pays off through larger harvests and the ability to save seeds for future growing seasons.
