Frequently Asked Questions

Will this bed last longer than treated wood raised beds?

Yes, significantly. Traditional treated wood lasts 8-15 years depending on climate and maintenance. The Suncast resin wicker shows no degradation after three months in full sun, and the manufacturer's design suggests 15-20+ year longevity. The resin doesn't rot, splinter, or require chemical treatments, making it genuinely low-maintenance compared to wood that needs regular sealing in harsh climates.

Is the 11-inch depth enough for vegetable gardening in June?

For most crops planted in June (peppers, eggplant, herbs, lettuce, green beans), 11 inches is adequate. Root depths for these summer crops typically max out at 8-12 inches. However, if you're growing root vegetables like carrots or parsnips, or want maximum tomato root development, you might prefer 12-18 inch depths. For June plantings specifically, 11 inches works well since these crops will mature before winter.

How does the resin wicker handle temperature extremes?

During my testing, I monitored the bed through temperature swings from 55°F to 92°F without any structural issues. The resin doesn't become brittle in cold or overly soft in heat like some plastics. The wicker material absorbs and dissipates heat well, actually keeping soil slightly cooler than dark wood frames—beneficial during hot June days when soil temperatures spike.

Do I need to add a landscape fabric or bottom liner?

I tested this both ways. Without fabric, ground-dwelling pests like slugs had direct access from below. Adding landscape fabric (basic $15-20 option) eliminated this problem while maintaining drainage. For the upfront investment you're making on this bed, adding quality fabric is worth the extra step.

How much soil does this 4x8x11 bed require?

You'll need approximately 32 cubic feet of soil mix. That's roughly 1.2 cubic yards or about 3 large bags if buying bagged soil (which gets expensive). I recommend calculating based on your local soil supplier's bulk pricing—buying in bulk typically costs 30-40% less than bagged options.
