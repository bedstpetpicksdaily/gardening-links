What to Avoid

  • Treated wood with chemical preservatives: Cheap pressure-treated lumber under $30 often contains harmful chemicals. While costs have improved, beginners shouldn't gamble with potentially contaminated soil where they're growing food. Spend a bit more on untreated or naturally rot-resistant wood.
  • Tiny decorative beds (2x2 or 2x3): These Instagram-worthy miniature beds look cute but frustrate beginners. They dry out too quickly, provide minimal growing space, and teach you bad habits. You'll either abandon gardening or immediately buy a second larger bed—wasting money and space.
  • Metal beds without drainage consideration: Some cheap galvanized steel beds get scorching hot in summer, literally cooking your plant roots. If choosing metal, ensure it's designed with ventilation or pick a shaded location. For beginners managing multiple variables, wood is simply easier.
