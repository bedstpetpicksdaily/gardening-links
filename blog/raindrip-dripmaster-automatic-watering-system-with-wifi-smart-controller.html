Frequently Asked Questions

Will this work with my existing drip irrigation lines?

Yes, if your lines use standard 1/2-inch or 5/8-inch fittings. However, the DripMaster works best as the central hub for a redesigned system rather than retrofitting old setups. If you're upgrading existing lines, expect to replace some connectors to ensure compatibility. The quick-connect design actually makes this easier than traditional buried systems.

How often do I need to replace batteries or worry about power issues?

The controller runs on either AC power (with battery backup) or solar-powered versions available. If you go with AC, the battery backup lasts through short outages but isn't meant for days-long power failures. Solar versions are genuinely useful in July when sun exposure is constant, though they're slightly more expensive upfront. Plan to check the battery status monthly during heavy use.

Can this handle both drip lines and traditional sprinklers?

Absolutely. You can run mixed setups with different water pressure requirements on different zones. I successfully ran low-pressure drip lines on one zone and higher-pressure pop-up sprinklers on another without needing additional equipment. Just make sure your water pressure supports all zones running simultaneously, or adjust your schedule to stagger them.

What's the real difference between this and a basic $30 timer?

A basic timer lets you set two or three fixed schedules. The DripMaster gives you 30+ zones with individual schedules, moisture sensors that adjust watering based on actual conditions, and remote access from anywhere. For a small container garden, spend the $30. For anything larger or if you travel, the smart system pays for itself in water savings within one season.
