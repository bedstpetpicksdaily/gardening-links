Frequently Asked Questions

What size vegetables can I grow in a 4x8 raised bed?

The 32 square feet of space accommodates full-sized tomato plants, squash, kale, carrots, lettuce, and peppers with proper spacing. A single 4x8 bed typically produces enough vegetables for a family of 4 throughout a growing season, depending on your climate and plant selection.

How deep should I fill this bed with soil?

Most vegetables perform optimally with 10-12 inches of quality garden soil. Root crops like carrots need at least 12 inches, while leafy greens work in 8 inches. With a 4x8 footprint, budget approximately 3.2 cubic yards of soil (roughly 12 bags of standard bagged soil).

Does this bed come with a bottom, or do weeds grow through?

The Gardman R687 is an open-bottom design, which actually prevents waterlogging and drainage problems. However, laying landscape fabric or hardware cloth underneath stops aggressive weeds and burrowing rodents from invading your growing space.
