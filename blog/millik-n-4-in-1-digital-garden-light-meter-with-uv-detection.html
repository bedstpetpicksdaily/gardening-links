Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the light intensity reading compared to professional equipment?

The Millikën measures in lux units and consistently reads within 10-15% of my reference meter, which is excellent for a consumer device. What matters more: these readings are repeatable. If your fussy orchid sits in a spot, measuring it three times will give nearly identical numbers. That reliability is what lets you track changes when you move plants or adjust shade cloth.

Can I use this to determine if my indoor grow lights are adequate?

Absolutely, and this is one of my favorite use cases. I measured my LED grow panels at seedling level and found they were delivering about 8,000 lux—perfect for most starts. The same meter checked my window sill at 15,000 lux on a bright July afternoon, showing why my south-facing plants were thriving. The UV measurement also helped me realize my grow lights emit minimal UV, which explained slower growth compared to outdoor hardened plants.

Is the UV detection feature worth the extra cost, or could I use a basic light meter instead?

If you grow photosensitive plants like lettuce, microgreens, or ornamental seedlings, UV data changes your decisions. High UV without protective diffusion can stunt or damage tender growth. I found myself using the UV reading at least twice weekly during summer to monitor if my shade cloth was filtering enough radiation. A basic meter would miss this entirely, and sunburned seedlings are expensive mistakes to learn from.
