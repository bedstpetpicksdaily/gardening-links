Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between bypass and anvil pruners?

Bypass pruners work like scissors with two curved blades that pass each other, creating clean cuts on living wood. Anvil pruners feature one sharp blade that cuts against a flat surface, making them better for dead wood. Garden Guru Tools offers both types to match your specific pruning needs.

How often should I sharpen my pruning tool blades?

Sharpen your pruning tools every 4-6 weeks during active growing season, or when you notice the blade is no longer cutting cleanly. Regular sharpening maintains efficiency, reduces plant damage, and extends tool lifespan significantly.

What size branches can pruners handle versus loppers?

Hand pruners typically cut branches up to 3/4 inch in diameter, while loppers handle branches up to 2 inches thick. For larger branches beyond 2 inches, a folding saw is your best option. Garden Guru Tools provides all three to cover every pruning scenario.

Are Garden Guru Tools suitable for professional landscapers?

Yes, Garden Guru Tools manufactures professional-grade hand tools trusted by landscapers and gardeners worldwide. Their equipment meets the durability and performance demands of regular commercial use while remaining accessible for home gardeners.

How do I prevent rust on my pruning tools?

Clean blades immediately after use, dry thoroughly, and store in a dry location. Apply light machine oil to metal parts monthly to prevent corrosion. Consider storing tools in a tool shed or garage rather than leaving them exposed to moisture.
