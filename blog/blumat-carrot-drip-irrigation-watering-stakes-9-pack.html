Frequently Asked Questions

How long does one reservoir fill last in July heat?

Depends entirely on stake depth, reservoir size, and soil type. In hot (85°F+), sandy conditions, expect 7-10 days. In moderate heat with denser soil, stretch to 14-21 days. Test your first setup and adjust. The ceramic cone itself lasts years; replaceable reservoirs ($2-5) are the consumable part.

Do I need to pair these with drip tubing or can they stand alone?

They work alone as standalone stakes or integrate with 1/4-inch drip tubing for larger garden beds. Standalone setup works best for containers and raised beds; tubing integration suits in-ground gardens or multiple stations. Both methods use the same ceramic regulation technology, just different distribution.

Will these work in potting soil that stays soggy?

Not reliably. Blumat stakes perform best in well-draining soil (peat, bark, perlite mixes). If your soil stays saturated for days, the capillary action never kicks in and the stake stays inactive. Test in a single pot first before committing all 9 stakes to a heavy-clay or poorly-draining bed.
