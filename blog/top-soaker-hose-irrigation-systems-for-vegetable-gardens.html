What to Look For

  • Emitter spacing and flow rate: Look for soaker hoses with emitters spaced 6-12 inches apart and delivering 0.5-1 gallon per hour per foot. Vegetables grow in compact spaces, so you need hoses flexible enough to weave between plants without creating permanent creases that block water flow.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long should I run a soaker hose for vegetable gardens?

    Most vegetable gardens need 1-2 inches of water per week, which typically requires running a soaker hose for 45 minutes to 1 hour, depending on water pressure and hose diameter. The best approach is to run it early morning for 30-60 minutes, then check soil moisture 6 inches deep—it should feel moist but not waterlogged. Adjust timing based on rainfall and your specific soil type, as sandy soil drains faster than clay.

    What size soaker hose do I need for my vegetable garden?

    For most home vegetable gardens, a 5/8-inch diameter soaker hose is ideal as it balances water flow and ease of installation, while 1/2-inch works for smaller gardens or raised beds. Calculate total garden length and add 20% extra for curves and connections, then choose between standard soaker hose (drips along entire length) or drip tape (more uniform spacing). For gardens under 500 square feet, a single 50-100 foot hose is usually sufficient.

    Do soaker hoses work on uneven or sloped garden beds?

    Soaker hoses work best on relatively level ground with less than a 5% slope, as steeper angles cause uneven water distribution with more flow at the lower end. On sloped gardens, lay hose lines horizontally across the slope rather than up-and-down, or use drip tape with built-in emitters for more consistent watering. For significant slopes, consider splitting your garden into zones with separate hose lines or using pressure regulators.

    How do I connect a soaker hose system to my outdoor faucet?

    Use a timer and backflow preventer directly on your faucet, then attach a hose connector to your soaker hose with hose clamps to prevent leaks. You'll also need an end cap or plug to seal the hose terminus so water flows through the soaker perforations rather than just running out the end. This entire setup typically costs $20-50 and takes less than 10 minutes to install.
