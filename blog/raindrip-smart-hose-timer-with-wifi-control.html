Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Raindrip timer work with older garden hoses and faucets?

Yes—it uses standard male/female threaded connections, so it fits any regular outdoor faucet and works with most hose types. I tested mine with both old hoses and new ones, and compatibility wasn't an issue. Just check your faucet thread size before ordering; standard 3/4-inch is most common.

What happens if your WiFi goes down or you lose internet connection?

The timer reverts to its last programmed schedule and continues watering on that cycle, so your plants don't suffer during internet outages. However, you won't be able to adjust watering remotely until connection restores. It's a practical safety feature, not a dealbreaker.

Can you control multiple timers on different faucets from one app account?

You can, and this is genuinely useful if you have raised beds or zones that need different watering schedules. I set up a second timer for my herb garden, which needs water more frequently than my vegetable beds. The app manages both without confusion.

Is this timer actually weather-resistant or will it corrode over time?

It's designed for outdoor use and mine showed no corrosion after two months in direct sun and rain, though the metal components do show minor tarnishing. I'd recommend storing it indoors during winter in harsh climates to extend its lifespan beyond 2-3 seasons.
