What to Look For

  • Thick, soft padding with ergonomic shaping: Look for grips made from foam, rubber, or gel materials that are at least 1.5 inches in diameter.   • Lightweight construction with proper weight distribution: Tools weighing under 2 pounds reduce stress on wrists and elbows. The weight should be balanced toward the head of the tool (shovel, pruner, etc.) so your hand and forearm aren't working against leverage. Heavier tools force your arthritic joints to work harder with every movement.
  • Non-slip, textured surfaces that don't require gripping: Cushioned grips should have a textured finish that provides friction without needing a death grip. This is especially important for wet or muddy gardening when arthritis sufferers might instinctively squeeze harder due to fear of dropping tools.
