Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to watering cans with screw-on rose heads?

Screw-on roses lock you into one spray pattern until you physically unscrew and swap the attachment—inconvenient when you're moving between delicate seedlings and established plantings. The one-touch toggle lets you switch mid-watering in a fraction of a second without setting the can down. For anyone managing mixed plantings, this time savings compounds across hundreds of waterings annually.

Is the 10-liter size big enough for large gardens?

A 10-liter can is ideal for container-heavy gardens and raised beds but will require multiple refills for large in-ground beds. If you have 100+ square feet of garden space, consider pairing this with a soaker hose system for efficiency. For containers and potted plants—which dominate most July watering routines—this capacity is near-perfect.

Does hard water damage the brass components or clog the nozzle?

Brass resists corrosion better than cheaper metals, but mineral deposits can accumulate in any nozzle over time. If you have hard water, flush the can with a vinegar solution monthly during heavy watering seasons. This takes five minutes and keeps the spray pattern crisp. Cheaper cans develop permanent mineral buildup that clogs; brass allows you to restore performance easily.
