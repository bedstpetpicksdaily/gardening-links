Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best automatic irrigation system for a small yard under 500 sq ft?

Drip irrigation kits and soaker hose systems are ideal for small yards because they deliver water directly to plant roots with minimal waste. For yards under 500 sq ft, a simple timer-based drip system (like Raindrip or DripWorks) typically costs $50-150 and covers most garden beds efficiently. These systems use 50% less water than sprinklers and can be installed in an afternoon without professional help.

How much does it cost to install an automatic irrigation system in a small yard?

DIY drip and soaker systems for small yards cost $40-200 depending on coverage area and water source, while smart sprinkler controllers run $100-300. Professional installation can add $300-800 in labor, but most small yard systems don't require professional installation. Basic timer-based setups are the most budget-friendly option at under $100.

Can I use an automatic irrigation system with well water or just city water?

Both well and city water work with automatic systems, though well water may require a filter to prevent clogs in drip lines. Soaker hoses and micro-sprinklers work well with either water source, but check your system's minimum water pressure requirements (usually 20-40 PSI). If you have low water pressure, a small booster pump ($100-200) may be needed.

What's the difference between soaker hoses and drip irrigation for small gardens?

Soaker hoses are cheaper and easier to install ($20-50 for a small yard) but less precise, while drip irrigation with emitters provides targeted watering and better water efficiency. Drip systems last 5-7 years versus 2-3 years for soaker hoses, making them more cost-effective long-term despite higher upfront costs. For small yards with mixed plantings, a combination of both works best.
