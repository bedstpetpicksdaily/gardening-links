Frequently Asked Questions

How many plants actually fit, and what size should they be?

Each tier holds 3-4 plants depending on pot size. The system works best with smaller starter plants (4-6 inches) or young seedlings that you can position close together. By midsummer, mature tomato or pepper plants will need thinning or relocation. Think herbs, strawberries, and lettuce rather than full-size eggplants. Realistic capacity is 12-15 plants for actual growing, not 25 as some listings suggest.

Does the watering system actually work, or is it just a gimmick?

It works, but it's not magic. Fill the reservoir at the top, and gravity distributes water through perforated tubes to lower tiers. In July heat, you'll still water every 1-2 days depending on soil and plant type. The benefit isn't eliminating watering—it's eliminating the hassle of individually watering 15 separate pots. One fill replaces 15 individual tasks. That said, some users report uneven moisture if the top soil compacts; using quality potting mix prevents this.

Will it blow over in wind, or stay stable?

The weighted base and low center of gravity keep it stable in typical residential wind. Gardeners in very windy areas (coastal regions, open plains) sometimes secure it with garden stakes or position it against a fence for extra safety. In normal suburban backyards, it's stable enough not to worry about. The real test is whether kids or pets can tip it—they can't, easily. Stability is one of the genuinely strong points here.
