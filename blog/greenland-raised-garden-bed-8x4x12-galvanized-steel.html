Frequently Asked Questions

Does the galvanized coating leach zinc into soil and make vegetables unsafe?

No. The USDA and EPA have confirmed galvanized steel in vegetable gardens is safe. The zinc coating is stable and won't contaminate soil at levels that affect plant safety. Thousands of commercial operations use galvanized beds without issues.

Will this bed handle the weight of 32 square feet of soil, water, and plants?

Yes. Galvanized steel at this gauge supports 800-1000+ pounds when properly assembled. Fully saturated soil with plants puts you around 400-600 pounds distributed evenly. The corner bracket system distributes this load safely.

How deep is 12 inches really for growing vegetables?

Sufficient for most vegetables. Tomatoes, peppers, squash, lettuce, and herbs thrive at 12 inches. Root vegetables like carrots and parsnips need at least 10 inches; this bed accommodates them fully. Only exceptionally deep-rooting perennials or fruit trees require 18+ inches.

Is assembly difficult if I'm not mechanically inclined?

No. The Greenland design uses interlocking corner brackets and bolts rather than welding or complex fastening. Follow the included instructions, use a basic wrench, and budget 45 minutes. YouTube has multiple assembly videos if you're visual learner.

Will this rust if I live in a humid climate?

Galvanized coating is specifically designed for humid environments. It lasts 10-15 years in standard climates. In salt-spray coastal areas, expect reduced longevity (5-8 years). Still superior to untreated metal, but not impervious to extreme conditions.
