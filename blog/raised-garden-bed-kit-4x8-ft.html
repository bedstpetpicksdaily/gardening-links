Frequently Asked Questions

What's the ideal depth for a 4x8 raised garden bed?

Most 4x8 kits feature 10-12 inch depths, ideal for vegetables. Root vegetables need 12+ inches; leafy greens work in 6-8 inches. Deeper beds accommodate more soil and provide better drainage.

Do I need to line the bottom of my raised bed?

A landscape fabric or hardware cloth lining prevents weeds and burrowing pests while allowing drainage. It's optional but recommended for easier maintenance.

How much soil does a 4x8 bed require?

A 4x8x12 inch bed needs approximately 32 cubic feet or 12 cubic yards of soil. Calculate based on your kit's specific dimensions.

Can metal raised beds get too hot?

Metal beds absorb heat but dissipate it effectively. Paint them light colors or add mulch to regulate temperature and protect plant roots.

What's the lifespan of a metal vs. wood raised bed?

Quality metal kits last 15-20+ years with minimal maintenance. Wood beds typically last 5-10 years depending on material and weather exposure.
