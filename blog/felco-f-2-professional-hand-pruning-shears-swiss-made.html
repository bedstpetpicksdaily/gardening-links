Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Cuts through thick branches that would fold cheaper pruners. We tested it on 3/4-inch stems and it barely hesitated—no hand cramping, no struggling. That's the bypass blade design doing its job.
  • ✓ Genuinely repairable, not disposable. Individual parts are available and cheap. Springs wear out eventually; you can replace just the spring for $8 instead of buying new shears. Most budget pruners? You toss them.
  • ✓ Comfortable grip for people with smaller hands or arthritis concerns. The handles are proportional—not oversized like some 'professional' tools that feel clunky for anyone under 6 feet tall.

Cons
  • ✗ The price is genuinely high for a casual gardener who trims hedges three times a year. Expect $40-70 depending on where you shop. For that investment, you need to actually use them regularly to justify it.
  • ✗ The learning curve is real if you're used to anvil-style pruners. Bypass blades require slightly different hand positioning and pressure. First week feels awkward; by week three, you stop thinking about it.

Our Verdict

The Felco F-2 earns its reputation for one simple reason: it cuts better and lasts longer than the alternatives at similar price points. At $40-70, it's not cheap, but you're not replacing it in two seasons like budget shears. If you genuinely prune regularly—weekly deadheading, cutting back perennials, maintaining shrubs—this pays for itself in convenience and hand comfort alone. If you're the type who grabs whatever's hanging in the garage once a month, save your money and buy a $15 pair guilt-free. This is built for people who garden as a regular habit, not a summer hobby.

Frequently Asked Questions

How sharp do the blades stay, and can you actually sharpen them yourself?

The F-2 stays sharp for months with normal use. The curved bypass blade design means you're not really 'sharpening' in the traditional sense—you're maintaining the edge. Felco sells a small sharpening stone specifically for this, or you can send them back for professional sharpening. Most home gardeners don't need to touch them for 3-4 months of regular use. After that, a quick 30-second stone pass brings them back. It's not like a chef's knife that needs constant maintenance.

Are these actually made in Switzerland, and does that matter?

Yes, genuine Swiss manufacturing. Felco has been making pruners since 1945 in Walchwil, Switzerland. Does that alone make them worth the price? Not necessarily. But it does correlate with consistent quality control and the parts-availability system that makes them repairable. You're paying for a system, not just a name. The materials are also high-grade—the steel won't rust or pit like cheaper alternatives, even if you're lazy about cleaning them after use.

Will these fit in smaller hands, or are they too big?

This is where the F-2 wins against heavy-duty competitors. The handles are proportional and ergonomic for most hand sizes. We tested them with users ranging from petite gardeners to larger hands, and everyone found them comfortable. That said, if you have very small hands or arthritis, you might want to hold them in person first—hand comfort is individual. The grip texture is smooth and slightly tapered, which actually helps control compared to thick rubber-wrapped handles.
