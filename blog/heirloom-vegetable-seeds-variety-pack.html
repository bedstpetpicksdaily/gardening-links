Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly are heirloom vegetable seeds?

Heirloom vegetables are open-pollinated plant varieties that have been passed down through generations, typically at least 40-50 years old. They're prized for exceptional flavor, unique colors, and the ability to save seeds for replanting.

Can I save seeds from heirloom vegetables?

Yes, one major advantage of heirloom seeds is that they're open-pollinated, meaning you can collect and save seeds from mature plants to grow next season, reducing long-term gardening costs.

Are heirloom seeds harder to grow than hybrid seeds?

Heirloom seeds aren't necessarily harder to grow, but they may require slightly more attention to soil quality, watering, and pest management for optimal results.

How many varieties typically come in a variety pack?

Most heirloom vegetable seed variety packs include 6-15 different vegetable varieties, with quantities ranging from 20-100 seeds per packet depending on the pack size.

What's the germination rate for heirloom seeds?

Quality heirloom seeds typically have germination rates of 75-90%. Always purchase from reputable suppliers and check expiration dates for best results.

Do heirloom seed packs work for container gardening?

Absolutely. Many heirloom varieties thrive in containers and raised garden beds, making them versatile for any gardening setup or space constraint.
