Frequently Asked Questions

How long do Traverseon coolers keep ice frozen?

Premium Traverseon coolers with advanced insulation can keep ice frozen for 5-7 days in moderate conditions, with some heavy-duty models extending to 10+ days when properly pre-chilled and maintained.

Are Traverseon coolers bear-proof?

While Traverseon coolers offer durable construction, they are not certified bear-proof. For bear country camping, we recommend using bear canisters or bear bags in addition to your Traverseon cooler for complete food storage security.

Can I use Traverseon coolers with their portable power stations?

Yes! Select Traverseon cooler models are compatible with their portable power stations, allowing you to power electric cooling features for extended trips or when maximum temperature control is needed.

What sizes do Traverseon camping coolers come in?

Traverseon offers coolers ranging from 20-quart compact models for solo camping to 100+ quart capacity models for large groups and extended expeditions. Most campers find 40-60 quart models ideal for family trips.

How do I maintain my Traverseon cooler?

Clean your cooler after each trip with mild soap and water, ensure the drain plug is clear, and store it in a cool, dry place. Avoid leaving it in direct sunlight for extended periods to preserve the exterior finish.
