Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 5.9ft handle compare to standard edger handles?

Standard edgers typically run 4.5-5 feet. This extra 14 inches means reduced bending during longer sessions, which I felt distinctly in my lower back after an hour of work. If you're over 5'10" or have any back considerations, the longer handle is worth factoring into your decision.

Can I use this with other Combisystem attachments?

Yes—that's the whole premise of the Combisystem. You can swap this edger head onto other Gardena handles (hedge trimmer handles, cultivators, etc.), making it genuinely modular. It's not true cross-compatibility with every brand, but Gardena's Combisystem is fairly extensive.

What soil types work best with this edger?

It performs best on loamy, established soil with moderate compaction. Sandy soil you'll cruise through; clay-heavy soil requires pre-watering and patience. Extremely hard, drought-compacted borders or new turf installations may need multiple passes or supplemental tools. July heat sometimes bakes soil too hard, so early morning use before the sun intensifies helps.

How often does the wheel need replacement?

On typical residential use (monthly edging maintenance), expect 2-3 seasons before noticing significant dulling. Hard soil or weekly use will shorten this. Replacement wheels are available separately and cost less than a new edger, but factor maintenance into your total cost of ownership.
