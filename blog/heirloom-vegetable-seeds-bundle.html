Frequently Asked Questions

What are heirloom vegetable seeds?

Heirloom seeds are open-pollinated varieties that have been passed down through generations. They produce plants that stay true to type, allowing you to save and replant seeds year after year while maintaining the plant's original characteristics.

Can I save seeds from heirloom vegetables?

Yes, saving seeds is one of the primary benefits of heirloom varieties. Since they're open-pollinated, you can collect mature seeds and replant them the following season. Keep varieties separated to prevent cross-pollination.

Are heirloom seeds suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. Many heirloom varieties are hardy and forgiving for new gardeners. Start with easy varieties like tomatoes, lettuce, and beans, then expand to more challenging types as your experience grows.

How should I store heirloom seeds?

Store seeds in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. An airtight container in a refrigerator or cool closet works well. Properly stored seeds can remain viable for 3-5 years or longer.
