Frequently Asked Questions

How long before galvanized steel rusts through?

Galvanized coating typically lasts 8-12 years depending on your climate and rainfall. In arid zones, you're looking at 12+. In wet climates, maybe 8-9. Minor surface rust is cosmetic and doesn't compromise structure. Real failure takes longer than most gardeners keep a single bed in one spot.

Do I need to line the bottom with landscape fabric?

Optional but smart. Fabric stops weeds from creeping up from native soil below, saving maintenance time. It doesn't drain poorly or trap water. Use basic landscape fabric—nothing fancy needed. Skip it if you're okay with weeding more.

Will this fit through a standard gate or doorway?

The 8-foot length won't bend around corners easily. Measure your path before delivery. If you're assembling on-site, bring the panels flat and bolt them where they'll live. Moving a fully assembled 8x4 bed through doorways isn't realistic unless you disassemble.

What soil amount do I actually need to fill this?

Roughly 4.2 cubic yards for full depth. In bags, that's about 28-30 bags of premium garden mix (depending on cubic feet per bag). Bulk soil delivery is usually cheaper—around $40-50 for a cubic yard if you can get a truck to your yard. Filling halfway is penny-wise and pound-foolish; shallow soil means stunted roots.

Can I use this for raised vegetables or just ornamentals?

Both work fine. The galvanized steel is food-safe for vegetables. No toxic coatings or treated wood chemicals leaching into soil. Plant anything you'd plant in a traditional garden—tomatoes, lettuce, herbs, root crops. The 17-inch depth is actually ideal for vegetable depth requirements.
