Frequently Asked Questions

What does full spectrum mean for LED grow lights?

Full spectrum refers to LED lights that emit wavelengths across the entire visible light range (400-700nm), mimicking natural sunlight and supporting all plant growth phases.

How far should LED grow lights be from plants?

Typical spacing is 12-24 inches depending on wattage and plant type. Check manufacturer recommendations and adjust based on leaf temperature and plant response.

Can LED grow lights replace natural sunlight completely?

Yes, full spectrum LEDs can completely replace natural sunlight for most indoor plants when properly positioned and operated for 12-16 hours daily.

How much electricity do LED grow lights use?

LED grow lights consume significantly less power than traditional options, typically using 30-100 watts depending on size and intensity, costing just pennies per day to operate.
