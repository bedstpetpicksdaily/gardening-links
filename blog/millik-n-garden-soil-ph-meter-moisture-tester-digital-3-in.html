Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the pH reading on the Millikén meter?

Accurate enough for practical gardening decisions. It won't replace a professional lab test for precise pH to one decimal place, but it catches the difference between neutral (6.5-7.0) and acidic (below 6) or alkaline (above 7.5) soil. For most home gardeners deciding whether blueberries or hydrangeas will thrive in a bed, that's exactly what you need. Readings stabilize after 10-15 seconds of probe insertion.

Does the moisture sensor work in both wet and dry soil?

Yes, but consistency matters. The sensor reads reliably between 10-60% soil moisture. If you test the same bed at the same time each week, you'll develop reliable baseline readings. Don't compare a reading from bone-dry soil to one from freshly watered soil and expect identical numbers—the point is tracking trends. For container plants, this tool eliminates overwatering guesswork entirely.

Will this meter last more than one season?

Yes. The metal probe won't corrode or bend after a season of weekly testing if you rinse it after use and store it dry (don't leave it wet in a dark shed). Battery life is 6-8 months with normal use, making it realistically good for a full growing season. Customers reporting failures after a few months typically left the meter in wet conditions or stored it powered on constantly.

Should I buy the Millikén over more expensive digital meters?

If you're deciding between this and meters over $100, know that you're not getting smartphone alerts or WiFi syncing for the extra money—you're mainly paying for brand premium. The Millikén's three-in-one function, durable probe, and reliable readings justify the mid-range price. Spend more only if you're testing dozens of beds professionally or you genuinely want app integration.
