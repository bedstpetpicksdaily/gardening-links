Frequently Asked Questions

What size fabric grow bag should I choose for my plants?

The 5-gallon size works well for most vegetables, herbs, and flowers. Use 3-gallon bags for smaller plants like lettuce and peppers, 7-gallon for tomatoes and potatoes, and 10-15 gallon for larger plants like peppers and root vegetables.

How long do fabric grow bags last?

Quality fabric grow bags typically last 3-5 seasons with proper care. Store them indoors during winter and avoid prolonged direct sunlight exposure to extend their lifespan significantly.

Can I use fabric grow bags for indoor gardening?

Yes, fabric grow bags work great indoors with grow lights and proper irrigation systems. They're commonly used for hydroponic and container gardening setups in basements and indoor growing spaces.

Do fabric grow bags need drainage holes?

No, fabric grow bags have built-in drainage through their breathable material, eliminating the need for separate drainage holes. This makes them ideal for raised beds and outdoor gardening.
