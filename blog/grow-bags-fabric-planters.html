Frequently Asked Questions

What size fabric grow bag should I use for vegetables?

For most vegetables, 5-gallon bags work well for herbs and leafy greens, 10-gallon bags suit tomatoes and peppers, and 20+ gallon bags are ideal for root vegetables and larger plants. Match the size to your plant's mature root system requirements.

How long do fabric grow bags last?

Quality fabric grow bags typically last 3-5 years with proper care. Storing them indoors during off-season, protecting from excessive UV exposure, and regular maintenance significantly extend their lifespan.

Can I reuse fabric grow bags?

Yes, fabric grow bags are highly reusable. Between seasons, rinse thoroughly with water, allow to dry completely, and store in a cool, dark location. Replace the soil for optimal plant health each growing season.

Do fabric grow bags need drainage holes?

Most quality fabric grow bags have built-in drainage throughout the fabric material itself. However, ensure you place them on a surface with proper drainage to prevent water pooling underneath the planters.
