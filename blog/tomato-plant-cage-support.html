Frequently Asked Questions

What size tomato cage do I need?

Choose a cage at least 48-60 inches tall for indeterminate varieties that grow throughout the season. Determinate types need 36-48 inch cages. Wider bases (28-30 inches) provide better stability.

When should I install the tomato cage?

Install cages at planting time or when seedlings are 6-12 inches tall. Early installation prevents root damage and allows plants to grow through support openings naturally.

Can tomato cages be used for other plants?

Yes, tomato cages work well for peppers, eggplants, and other tall vegetables. They're also suitable for supporting flowering plants and herbs in garden beds.

How do I prevent tomato cages from rusting?

Choose powder-coated or galvanized steel cages designed for outdoor use. Store indoors during off-season and rinse regularly to remove debris and moisture.
